Many medicines can cause diarrhoea, and the following list is not exhaustive.

- Antibiotics by reducing the intracolic fermentation of carbohydrates, resulting in osmotic diarrhoea,

- A proton pump inhibitor (lansoprazole), non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs, serotonin reuptake inhibitors, venotonics: microscopic colitis,

- Colchicine: secretory diarrhoea,

- Metformin: increased hepatic synthesis of bile acids,

- Olmesartan and mycophenolate mofetil: enteropathy with villous atrophy,

- Immunotherapy (anti-CTLA-4, anti-PD1): autoimmune colitis with digestive exudation.
