'''In adults and children'''

The diagnosis is clinical,

Sudden onset of an inflammatory placard associated with general signs.

Local signs: acute appearance of an ''inflammatory placard'' (erythema, heat, oedema, pain), progressively spreading over several days and ''well limited''; presence of non-haemorrhagic bullae or purpura also reported; ''locoregional adenopathy'' or ''lymphangitis (Figure 10)''.

General signs: high fever (38.5° to 40°C), chills, feeling unwell
