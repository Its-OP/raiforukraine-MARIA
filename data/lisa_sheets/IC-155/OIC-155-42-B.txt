Depends on the aetiology of the facial cellulitis.

'''Non-necrotizing bacterial dermohypodermatitis of the face'''

Management identical to that for non-necrotising bacterial dermohypodermatitis of the lower limbs.


'''Facial cellulitis of dental origin'''

From the collected stage, medical-surgical emergency

Broad-spectrum antibiotic therapy covering "Staphylococcus aureus", "Streptococcus pyogenes" and anaerobes ("Prevotella", "Fusobacterium", actinomycetes etc.).

Preventive or curative anticoagulation if associated thrombophlebitis.

Surgical management of the entry site and removal of necrotic tissue where appropriate


'''Staphylococcie maligne de la face'''

Medical emergencies

IV antibiotic therapy for Staphylococcus aureus

Curative anticoagulation depending on facial vein involvement.
