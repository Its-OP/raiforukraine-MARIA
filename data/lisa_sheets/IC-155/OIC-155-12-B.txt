|'''Non-necrotizing bacterial dermohypodermatitis''''
|'''Bacterial necrotizing dermohypodermatitis'''
|-
|Epidemiology
|'''In adults,''''

Estimated incidence in France between 10 and 100 cases per 100,000 inhabitants

Predominantly female

Average age 60-65

In children,

occurs in previously healthy, relatively young children under 3 years of age
<br />
|'''In adults,''''

Incidence estimated at 0.4/100,000 per capita, with an increase after the age of 50.


In children,

Prevalence estimated at 0.8 per million patients per year
|-
|Risk factors
|'''In adults,''''

Local: leg ulcer,

inter-toe intertrigo,

wound, lymphoedema (lymphatic insufficiency),

venous insufficiency,

history of erysipelas


'''Generals'''

obesity

<br />
|'''In adults,''''

advanced age

associated comorbidities: diabetes, heart failure, hepatocellular insufficiency, renal insufficiency, obesity, male


'''In children,'''

- cutaneous route of entry: trauma, bite

- chickenpox

- immunosuppression
