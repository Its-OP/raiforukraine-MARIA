'''Local signs'''

- Intense pain, beyond any measure, beyond the erythematous, oedematous and infiltrated zone; functional impotence

- crepitation, cyanotic livedoid areas, skin necrosis, hypoesthesia or anaesthesia

- very rapid spread (a few hours)

- worsening of local signs after 48 hours of well-administered antibiotic therapy

'''General signs'''

Signs of sepsis: hypotension (hypotension is a late sign in children, tachycardia is a better early sign), tachycardia, confusion (irritability or drowsiness in infants), polypnoea.
