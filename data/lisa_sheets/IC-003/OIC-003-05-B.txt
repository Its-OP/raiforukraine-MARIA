- Often multiple reasons for consultation (2.6 per consultation on average).

- Intriguing follow-up of chronic pathologies and acute pathologies.

- The doctor must adapt his handling of the various consultation results to the patient's "degree of urgency" and "priorities". He ''helps himself to time''.

- Doctors should bear in mind the "danger of check-ups and of enrolling patients in unreasonable care pathways". This is "quaternary prevention".
