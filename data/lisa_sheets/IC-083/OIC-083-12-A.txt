#'''<u>Collyres anaesthetics</u>''''

'''Example:''' oxybuprocaine, tetracaine

'''Indications :''' temporary anaesthesia for diagnostic procedures (e.g. aplanation tonometry, gonioscopy, corneal sampling) or therapeutic procedures (e.g. abundant eye washing after chemical burns, intravitreal injection, phacoemulsification under topical anaesthesia).


'''Contraindications:''' formal prohibition of prolonged use which would cause serious irreversible corneal epithelial damage, hypersensitivity to one of the components


2. '''<u>Collyres antibiotics</u>''''

'''Example:''' tobramycin, ciprofloxacin, azythromycin (commercial formulations); ticarcillin, gentamicin, amikacin, vancomycin (reinforced eye drops prepared in hospital pharmacies for hourly instillation in infectious keratitis).

Indications: '''Bacterial conjunctivitis, infectious bacterial keratitis, post-operative treatment (all intraocular surgery)

<u>''Warning:''</u> prescribing antibiotic eye drops is "not automatic". The prescription of an antibiotic is reserved for the treatment of bacterial infections


'''Contraindications:'''hypersensitivity to one of the components, class-specific contraindications (e.g. pregnant or breast-feeding woman for tetracyclines; child < 6 months for chloramphenicol)


3. '''<u>Corticosteroids</u>''''

'''Example:''' dexamethasone, alone or in combination with antibiotics (example: tobramycin/dexamethasone)

'''Indications:'''' intraocular inflammation (acute anterior uveitis, post-operative after-effects of all intraocular surgery), allergic conjunctivitis


'''Contraindications:''' dendritic herpetic keratitis (herpetic corneal ulcer), mycotic (fungal) keratitis, viral keratoconjunctivitis (in the acute phase), ocular hypertonia (precaution but not a formal contraindication), hypersensitivity to one of the ingredients.
