The main aetiologies to be considered in the presence of a painful red eye with reduced visual acuity are:

- keratitis

- acute glaucoma

- acute anterior uveitis

- if post-operative: infectious endophthalmitis (most often after cataract surgery or intravitreal injection)

A [[:Fichier:Oeil rouge CAT.jpg|arbre d'orientation diagnostique]] for red eye is provided in the [[Conduite à tenir devant un œil rouge douloureux sans baisse d'acuité visuelle 2C-083-PC-A01]] sheet.

Broadly speaking, the guiding principles and key points in the management of a painful red eye with reduced visual acuity are as follows:

- reduced corneal transparency, perikeratic circle, fluorescent ulceration + = keratitis; no local corticoids without a precise diagnosis;

- miosis, perikeratic circle, clear cornea, iridocrystalline synechiae, retrodescemetic precipitates = anterior uveitis; mydriatic treatment and local corticoids + etiological assessment;

- unilateral blindness, pain +++, digestive syndrome, mydriasis, corneal oedema, increased intraocular pressure +++ = acute glaucoma; treatment of hypertonia.

- postoperative inflammation requires urgent examination and treatment (suspicion of postoperative endophthalmitis++).[[File:Oeil rouge CAT.jpg|alt=Démarche diagnostique devant un œil rouge|vignette|680x680px|Démarche diagnostique devant un œil rouge]]
