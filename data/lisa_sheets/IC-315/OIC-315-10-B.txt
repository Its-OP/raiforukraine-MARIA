'''Impairment of 2 or 3 blood lines'' (anaemia, thrombocytopenia, neutropenia) on the haemogram requires a myelogram before any specific treatment as it suggests a bone marrow cause for the cytopenias. Circulating blastosis is not necessary for a myelogram to be indicated.

This will show > 25% blasts on rich MO in the case of acute leukaemia. This examination should be systematically supplemented by CSF analysis, even in the absence of any neurological symptoms.
