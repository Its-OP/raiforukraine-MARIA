'''<big>The clinical examination may initially provide little information and must therefore be thorough, complete and repeated.</big>''''

<big>The clinical examination must include a careful examination of the following points, in an undressed patient:</big>

<big>- '''Constants:''' Blood pressure, heart rate, respiratory rate, temperature, Glasgow score, HaemoglocuTest, diuresis: '''to quickly detect signs of severity'''</big>

<big>- '''Interrogatory: onset of pain (thoracic, adominal), urinary or digestive functional signs, respiratory functional signs (cough, sputum ...), etc.'' </big>

<big>- '''Pulmonary auscultation (appearance of a focus of crepitus?)'''</big>

<big>- '''Cardiac auscultation (appearance of a murmur?)'''</big>

<big>- '''Examination of the integuments: the entire skin covering and mucosa (buccal and genital)'''</big>

<big>- '''Examination of intravenous catheter insertion points'''</big>

<big>- '''Urinary catheter examination'''</big>

<big>- '''Abdominal palpation (pain/defensiveness/contraction, meteorism, tympany)'''</big>

<big>- '''Thorough examination of the perineum'''</big>
