The three main infectious causes of morbilliform exanthema are:

- measles (unvaccinated children and adults)

- parvovirus B19 infection (megalerythema epidemic) (children aged 5-10)

- infectious mononucleosis (EBV) (adolescents, young adults)

In addition, maculopapular toxidermia is a frequent cause of morbilliform exanthema in children and adults.

Other infectious aetiologies (bacterial, viral or parasitic): very varied and generally associated with extracutaneous manifestations (ENT, digestive, etc): echovirus, adenovirus, primary HIV infection, dengue fever, enterovirus, toxoplasmosis,

rickettiosis, leptospirosis, etc.
