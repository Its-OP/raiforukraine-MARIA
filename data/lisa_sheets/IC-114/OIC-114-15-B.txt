The 4 main causes of exanthema in pregnant women associated with a foetal risk are as follows:

- rubella in non-immune women (risk of foetal death or malformations)

- toxoplasmosis in non-immune women (risk of congenital toxoplasmosis)

- parvovirus B19 infection in non-immune women (risk of hydrops if infection occurs in the 2nd trimester)

- syphilis (risk of congenital syphilis)
