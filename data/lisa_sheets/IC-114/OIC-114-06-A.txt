Systemic complications of erythroderma can include :

- hydro-electrolytic disorders (dehydration ++), protein deficiencies and deficiencies in certain vitamins and minerals

trace elements (iron, zinc, vitamins, etc.)

- thermoregulation disorders

- decompensation of chronic diseases, particularly metabolic diseases (diabetes),

cardiovascular (haemodynamic disorders), pulmonary, renal

- infectious bacterial and viral complications, particularly those originating in the urinary tract

and pulmonary

- complications of prolonged decubitus (pressure sores, malnutrition, deep vein thrombosis, etc.)

- complications related to etiological treatments for erythroderma
