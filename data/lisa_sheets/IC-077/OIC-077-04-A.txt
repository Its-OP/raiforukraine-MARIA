'''4. Withdrawal syndrome and complications of withdrawal''''

4.1 Benzodiazepine withdrawal syndrome (ICD-10 criteria) occurs when benzodiazepines are stopped abruptly, whether accidentally or not (forgetfulness, hospitalisation, etc.).

The severity of the withdrawal syndrome is greater when :

<nowiki>*</nowiki> the decrease in dosage is rapid (no plateau) ;

<nowiki>*</nowiki> the dose consumed of benzodiazepines is high;

<nowiki>*</nowiki> if the benzodiazepine has a short half-life;

<nowiki>*</nowiki> if there is significant anxiety about stopping the substance;

<nowiki>*</nowiki> if there is a co-morbid characterised depressive episode;

<nowiki>*</nowiki> if there is regular overconsumption of alcohol or another psychoactive substance.

'''The symptoms of benzodiazepine withdrawal are:'''

<nowiki>*</nowiki> Tremors, anxiety, insomnia, headaches.

<nowiki>*</nowiki> More specific signs: confusion, hallucination.

<nowiki>*</nowiki> More rarely: impaired alertness, convulsions, motor incoordination, coma.

'''When do the symptoms appear?'''

           * may occur as soon as the dosage is reduced (not necessarily when the benzodiazepines are stopped)

           * duration of withdrawal depends on the drug used;

           * Symptoms may improve over several days.
