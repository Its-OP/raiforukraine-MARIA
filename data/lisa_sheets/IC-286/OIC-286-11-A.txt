Clostridioides difficile infection or colitis '''without signs of severity''', treatment '''at home''' :

- Stop the antibiotic responsible if possible

- Oral hydration and hygiene measures, loperamide contraindicated

- Antibiotic therapy with oral vancomycin for 10 days. Alternatives possible with fidaxomicin or metronidazole for 10 days.

- No need to check the stool culture at the end of treatment.

Clostridioides difficile'' colitis with ''signs of severity and/or fragile terrain'', inpatient treatment:

- Stop the antibiotic responsible if possible

- Intravenous hydration and correction of fluid and electrolyte disorders

- Contact isolation and hygiene measures

- Contraindicate loperamide.

- Antibiotic treatment with oral vancomycin for 10 days, with the option of combining with intravenous metronidazole.

- Rarely: emergency surgical treatment is possible (subtotal colectomy) in cases of colectasis, toxic megacolon or colonic perforation (50% mortality).

- No need to check the stool culture at the end of treatment

Treatment of recurrences of Clostridioides difficile colitis (10-20% of cases)

- Oral vancomycin or fidaxomicin for 10 days

- From the 2nd<sup>e</sup> recurrence (3rd<sup>e</sup> episode), recourse to faecal microbiota transplantation is indicated with over 85% success.
