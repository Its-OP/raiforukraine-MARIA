'''In case of hypereosinophilia > 1 500/mm<sup>3</sup> (invasive phase) (serological diagnosis)''''hepatic distomatosis (''Fasciola hepatica'', ingestion of watercress) :angiocholitis picture with jaundice and '''fever''' '''([[Hyperthermia/fever SD-044|fever/hyperthermia]])'''' ;

- scabies

- trichinosis or trichinellosis (''Trichinella spiralis'', ingestion of undercooked pork, wild boar or horse meat): ''fever'' '''([[Hyperthermia/fever SD-044|fever/hyperthermia]])''', oedema and '''[[Myalgia SD-077|myalgia]]'''; a muscle biopsy may be useful

'''In cases of more moderate eosinophilia (< 1,500/mm<sup>3</sup>, parasitoses without tissue cycle) ('''parasitological examinations of stools (taeniasis) or serologies (anisakiasis, hydatidosis and echinococcosis))

- anisakiase or anisakidosis (''Anisakis'', ingestion of raw fish): digestive signs.

- hydatidosis (''Echinococcus granulosus'', ingestion of food or water contaminated with dog faeces): hydatid cysts in the liver or other organs. Rupture/cracking of a hydatid cyst may also be accompanied by a hypereosinophilia > 1500/mm<sup>3</sup>.

- alveolar echinococcosis (''Echinococcus multilocularis'', in eastern France).
