'''Emergencies related to visceral lesions of eosinophilia:'''

- respiratory failure '''([[Acute respiratory distress SD-160|acute respiratory distress]])''' ;

- neurological failure (sometimes of arterial thrombotic origin) ;

- heart failure (endomyocardial fibrosis).

'''Emergencies linked to the need to identify the cause quickly:'''

- parasitosis (Strongyloides infection or malignant anguillulosis, particularly in immunocompromised patients);

- GEPA (myocarditis, intra-alveolar haemorrhage, rapidly progressive glomerulonephritis);

- drug hypersensitivity syndrome (or DRESS, for ''Drug Reaction with Eosinophilia and Systemic Symptoms'') (''[[Suspicion of an adverse drug reaction or SD-348 care|suspicion of an adverse drug reaction]]'').
