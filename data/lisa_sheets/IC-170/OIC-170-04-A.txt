'''<big>Any malaria attack can progress to a severe attack and the death of the patient</big>''''

Plasmodium falciparum is responsible for almost all severe malaria attacks

Patients at risk of a severe attack are those of extreme age (children, the elderly), pregnant women and patients with splenectomies or a non-functioning spleen, with co-morbidities or immunosuppressed.

<br />The presence of signs of severity requires close monitoring and appropriate management, ideally in a continuing care unit.
