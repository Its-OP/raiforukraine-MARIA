Obstetric violence refers to practices carried out voluntarily or involuntarily by medical staff, which are violent or are experienced as such by the woman during childbirth. '''[[Screening and prevention of violence against women SD-317|Screening and prevention of violence against women]]''''

==Type of violence
7 types of violence can be distinguished:

#Physical violence
#sexual violence
#verbal abuse
#stigmatisation and discrimination (sexism, racism, homophobia, grossophobia, etc.)
#non-compliance with care standards (inappropriate acts) or non-consent
#poor relations between women and carers
#poor conditions and constraints due to the health system or its organisation

==Consequences
They have short-term consequences

- Pain, burning, cramps and paresthesias SD-073|Pain]]

- Fears

And in the long term

- Post-traumatic stress disorder,

- Fear of childbirth or gynaecological care

- Negative body image

- Feeling of dehumanisation
