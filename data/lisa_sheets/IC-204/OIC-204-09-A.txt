In practice, symptomatic cough medicines have little place in the management of acute or chronic cough. Priority is given to identifying the aetiological factor(s). Their benefit/risk ratio is low. Scientific proof of their efficacy has yet to be demonstrated, and the side-effects are sometimes significant.

* Central-acting cough suppressants :
** Narcotic opiates: codeine, pholcodine
** Non-narcotic opiates: dextromethorphan, noscapine
** Anti-histamines: alimenazine, prometazine
** Non-opioid, non-antihistamines: oxeladine, pentoxyverine, clobutinol
* Peripherally acting cough suppressants:
** Amylein hydrochloride, aconitine, sodium benzoate.
