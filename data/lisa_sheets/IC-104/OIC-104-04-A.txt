|Title=Knowing the signs of retrobulbar optic neuritis (RONB)
|Description=None
|Rubric=Positive diagnosis
|Contributors=
|Order=4}}
- Subacute decrease in visual acuity ([[Vision abnormality SD-138|Vision abnormality]]).

- Periorbital pain, made worse by moving the eye.

- No red eyes.

- Scotoma central or paracentral to the visual field.

- Dyschromatopsia (colour vision disorder).

- Normal fundus, rare papilledema. Discolouration of the papilla may occur at a distance from the episode.

- Recovery of full visual function in 80% of cases within 6 months.
