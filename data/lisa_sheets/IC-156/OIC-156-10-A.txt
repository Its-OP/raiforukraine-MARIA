|Order=10}}
- Location: monoarticular (80%), knee (50%) and hip most often

- Sudden onset

- [[Hyperthermia/fever SD-044|Fever/hyperthermia]] (50%)

- [[Joint pain SD-067|Joint pain]] and functional impotence (85%), analgesic attitude, [[Lameness SD-068|lameness]], [[Appearance of difficulty in walking SD-066|appearance of difficulty in walking]]

- Mobilisation of the extremely painful joint

- Joint effusion (patellar shock for the knee, for example) and inflammatory signs (clinically assessable for superficial joints)

- Systematic search for signs of severity (sepsis), a portal of entry, other secondary sites of bacteremia, signs of infective endocarditis, etc.
