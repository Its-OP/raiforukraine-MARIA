'''Cannabis''' :

- Psychoactive principle responsible for the psychotropic effects of cannabis: Delta9-THC

- Action on CB1 and 2 brain receptors

- Effects dependent on dose, route of administration and individual factors

'''Cocaine''':

- 2 forms: white powder (hydrochloride) or solid base (pebbles, cakes) called crack or "free base" obtained by adding sodium bicarbonate (crack) or ammonia (freebase) to cocaine hydrochloride.

'''Amphetamines''' :

- Various synthetic drugs: amphetamine (speed, pill, pilou, peanut), methamphetamine (speed, meth, crystal meth, ice, tina, yaba; in the form of inhalable crystals, tablets or powder), MDMA (powder or crystal form) or ecstasy (tablet form).

.       MDMA: psychoactive principle of ecstasy/MDMA

'''Opiates/opioids:'''

- Opiates are a family of products obtained from opium, a natural product derived from poppy crops (morphine, heroin, codeine). Opioids (which include opiates) are agonists of the receptors on which opiates act and include (among others) methadone, high-dose buprenorphine, tramadol, oxycodone, fentanyl, etc.

'''New synthetic products''' (NPS):

<nowiki>.     Cathinones: Méphédrone, MDPV, Methylone; 4-MEC and 3-MMC (the most spotted online), Alpha-PVP, NRG [mixture of cathinones]...) </nowiki>

.    Synthetic cannabinoids: substances classified as narcotics in France since 2009. Nearly half of the NPS sold contain neither tobacco nor cannabis, and are inhaled.
