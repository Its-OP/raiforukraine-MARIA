'''Cannabis''':

- Onset of effects: 15-20 minutes after use

- Duration between 3 and 6 hours

- Conjunctival hyperhaemia, increased appetite, dry mouth, tachycardia

- Moderate euphoria, well-being, sedation,

- Subjective sensation of time slowing down, changes in sensory perceptions, ideas of reference, impaired motor coordination,

- Cognitive disorders (memory, attention, concentration, executive functions)

- Possible anxiety

'''Cocaine:'''

'''Manic clinical picture''' with :

- euphoria; feeling of well-being, increased energy, delusions of grandeur, tachypsychia, disinhibition, hypervigilance, biased improvement in concentration, anorexia, increased sexual interest and arousal, insomnia

- neurovegetative signs: tachycardia, hypertension, mydriasis, skin pallor

After the effects of cocaine wear off, there is a transitional phase of hedonic dysregulation ("descent") characterised by dysphoria, asthenia, irritability, loss of self-esteem and anxiety.

Management of this phase by the use of other substances (alcohol, benzodiazepines, opioids, cannabis, etc.), which can lead to secondary addictive disorders.

'''Amphetamines:'''

'''Stimulating clinical picture''' with :

- Euphoria, a feeling of well-being, increased self-confidence

- Increased attention and concentration

- Tachypsychia

- Increased sexual desire;

- Neuro-vegetative signs: hypertension, tachycardia, increased respiratory rate

For ecstasy/MDMA users: 2 major effects initially with euphoria and empathy in addition to the overall clinical picture - For methamphetamine, the effects are rapid as with crack cocaine
