- Substitutive treatment for major opiate dependence as part of medical, social and psychological care.

- Duration: a few months, a few years or for life, depending on the patient's degree of dependency and choice of care.

- 3 treatments in France: methadone hydrochloride (syrup, capsules); high-dose buprenorphine (Subutex® or generics) (tablets); combination of high-dose buprenorphine and naloxone (Suboxone®) (tablets).

- Prescription as part of an overall care protocol

- Methadone: Prescription initiated in a healthcare establishment or specialist care centre on a secure prescription (max. 14 days for the syrup and max. 28 days for the capsule). Relay provided by general practitioners

- high-dose buprenorphine: prescription by any doctor on a secure prescription (28 days max)

- Split delivery (daily, weekly, etc.)

- The aim is to stop the misuse of opiates and reduce their consumption, with a view to reducing risk and harm. This

- Therapeutic framework combining control of delivery, urinary dosages and appropriate dosage
