'''The diagnosis is clinical.'''

'''Clinical elements to look for'''

==1. In adults===
Involvement of the face: most frequent topography. Erythematous patches covered with scales in seborrheic areas: nasolabial folds, eyebrows, glabella, anterior edge of the scalp, etc.

Involvement of the scalp :'''' Sometimes isolated, non-adherent scales (dandruff). Sometimes severe forms with scaly helmet

Involvement of the trunk: annular or circular lesions with a scaly border predominating in the median pre-sternal area

==2. In newborns and infants ==
Starts after birth, most often with yellow scabs on the scalp (cradle cap) and face. May affect the breech area. Rarely erythroderma.

Severe forms: severe seborrhoeic dermatitis resistant to treatment should be tested for HIV.
