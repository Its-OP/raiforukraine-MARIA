'''The diagnosis is clinical.'''

'''Clinical elements to look for:''''

=== 1. vascular form ===

==== Paroxysmal phenomena: flushing in response to variable stimuli (heat, alcohol, spices) ====

==== Permanent phenomena: erythematotelangiectatic rosacea ====
Facial erythema and telangiectasias affecting the cheeks, nose, chin and forehead

=== 2. Papulo-pustular form: papules and pustules without comedones. ===

=== 3. Hypertrophic form: rhinophyma ===
=== 4. Ocular form ===

0-50% of rosaceas are accompanied by ocular involvement. It may precede the cutaneous form of rosacea by several years. It takes the form of ''conjunctivitis, blepharitis, keratitis, chalazions, telangiectasias of the eyelids''.
