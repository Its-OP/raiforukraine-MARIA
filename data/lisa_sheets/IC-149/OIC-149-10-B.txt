'''Complications of streptococcal angina''' :

- post-streptococcal syndromes: acute rheumatic fever (RAA) and acute post-streptococcal glomerulonephritis (GNA), which have become exceptional in France

- streptococcal toxin complications: scarlet fever and streptococcal toxic shock

'''Complications of pseudomembranous angina''' :

- Complications of infectious mononucleosis: haemolytic anaemia, thrombocytopenic purpura, neurological damage, ruptured spleen, etc.

- Diphtheria and its cardiac and neurological complications

'''Complications of vesicular angina:''' '''Severe herpetic gingivo-stomatitis''' preventing feeding.

'''Complications of ulcerative and ulcero-necrotic angina''': '''Vincent's angina''' can be complicated by a pulmonary angina-infarction syndrome '''or Lemierre syndrome'''.
