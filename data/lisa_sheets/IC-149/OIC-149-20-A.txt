When of '''bacterial''' origin, they are most often due to :

- at ''''association fuso-spirillaire : Vincent's angina (painful ulceration)''''

- more rarely '''''treponema pallidum''''': '''syphilitic chancre (painless ulceration)'''

Other rarer causes of ulcerative necrotizing angina include ENT cancer and haematological malignancy.
