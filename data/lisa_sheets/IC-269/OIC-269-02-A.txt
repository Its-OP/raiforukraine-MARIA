There are many aetiologies in each age group. They cannot be summarised in a few lines. In this chapter, we can mention those ''which are life-threatening in the short term'' (considering the main ones, in decreasing order of frequency):

Before 1 month: Strangulated inguinal hernia, small bowel volvulus, ulcerative colitis (premature), Hirschsprung's disease

1 month to 2 years: acute intestinal invagination, strangulated inguinal hernia, abdominal trauma, small bowel volvulus

2 to 12 years: appendicitis and appendicular peritonitis, sickle cell crisis, diabetes, etc.
