There are markers for these urgent pathologies, which can be treated medically or surgically.

- When there are signs of vital distress: respiratory, circulatory or neurological.

- When the pain is severe (measured on an appropriate scale), and even more so when it is difficult to relieve

- When these pathologies affect a child at risk (premature baby, newborn, disabled child or child with a chronic illness)

- In special circumstances: high-kinetic trauma, penetrating wound, etc.

- Warning signs: bilious vomiting, melena, abdominal tenderness and contracture, inguinal swelling, etc.
