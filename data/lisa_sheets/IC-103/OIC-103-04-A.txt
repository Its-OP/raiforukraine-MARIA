Diagnosis is essentially based on <nowiki/>'''interrogation''', which must be meticulous and directive in order to clarify:

#'''Characteristics of [[Vertigo and vertiginous sensation SD-064|vertigo]]''':
#*confirm vertigo ?
#*rotatory ? linear displacement ?
#*or instability, pitching, balance disorders?
#'''Duration of attack:''''
#*a few seconds (e.g. BPPV - benign paroxysmal positional vertigo)
#*several hours or minutes (e.g. Ménière's disease)
#*several days
#Mode of onset:''''
#*spontaneous, not triggered by movement
#*triggered by movement (change of position, rotational movement of the head) e.g. BPPV
#*Triggered by noise (Tullio phenomenon) or nose blowing e.g. labyrinthine fistula
#'''Evolution, repetition:''''single or repeated attacks? permanent vertiginous sensation? in 2 tps with free interval (stroke)?
#'''Accompanying signs'' :
#*<u>[[Decreased hearing/deafness SD-140|hypoacusis]], tinnitus, ear fullness</u> (Menière), uni or bilateral
#*<u>Neurovegetative tr.</u> : [[Nausea SD-012|nausea]], [[Vomiting SD-013|vomiting]], pallor, [[Hypersudation SD-025|sueurs]], accelerated transit ([[Diarrhoea SD-002|diarrhoea]])
#*<u>[[Headache SD-118|headache]]</u>: look for migraines and intracranial hypertension
#*<u>[[Neck pain SD-144|cervicalgia]]</u>: often related to [[Balance disorder SD-130|balance disorder]] (neck muscle contractions). Intense and unilateral. Beware of vertebral artery dissection +++.
#*if dysmetria, [[Sensory and/or motor neurological deficit SD-121|motor or sensory disorders]], [[Diplopia SD-143|diplopia]], [[Visual abnormality SD-138|low visual acuity]], cranial pair involvement: attention NEURO CAUSE
#Antecedents: otological, cardiovascular, head trauma, migraine, ototoxic or psychotropic treatments.
