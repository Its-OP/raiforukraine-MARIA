#'''<u>ACUTE VESTIBULAR SYNDROME:</u>''''

ISOLATED ACUTE VESTIBULAR SYNDROME

Vestibular neuritis

- Stroke (posterior fossa)

ACUTE VESTIBULAR SYNDROME ACCOMPANIED by otological signs (deafness, tinnitus, otorrhea, etc.)

- first attack of Menière's disease

- otological disorders (otitis, cholesteatoma, rock tumours)

ACUTE VESTIBULAR SYNDROME ACCOMPANIED by neurological signs

- STROKE

- Tumours

- MS


'''2. <u>RECURRENTVERTIGES</u>''''

- Lasting from a few minutes to a few hours: Menière's disease, vestibular migraines

- For less than a minute: BPPV

Note that a vestibular schwannoma can take any of these clinical forms
