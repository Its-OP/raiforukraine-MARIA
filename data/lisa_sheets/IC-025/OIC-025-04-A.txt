"Any woman of childbearing age until proven otherwise".


Questioning :

- Identification of risk factors

- Search for delayed menses (DDR?) / unusual menses

- Search for functional signs:

o Pelvic pain, lateralized or not '''([[Pelvic pain SD-099|pelvic pain]], [[Abdominal pain SD-004|abdominal pain]], [[Lumbar region pain SD-036|lumbar region pain]], [[Assessment and management of acute pain SD-259|assessment and management of acute pain]])''''

o Metrorrhagia (dark and scanty, classically "sepia") '''([[Genital bleeding during pregnancy SD-111|genital bleeding during pregnancy]], [[Abnormal genital bleeding (outside known pregnancy) SD-112|abnormal genital bleeding outside known pregnancy]], [[Acute haemorrhage SD-060|acute haemorrhage]])'''''

o Sympathetic signs of pregnancy (nausea, vomiting, breast tension)

o Scapular pain, dizziness, malaise, agitation, anxiety '''([[Palpitations SD-165|palpitation]], [[Tachycardia SD-166|tachycardia]], [[Malaise/loss of consciousness SD-050|malaise loss of consciousness]])''''


May (rarely) be asymptomatic.


Physical examination :

- Vitantes / Etat hémodynamique : recherche signes de choc hémorragique / saignement : premier signe : tachycardie sinusale +++) '''([[Découverte d'une hypotension artérielle SD-043|découverte d'une hypotension artérielle]], [[Tachycardie SD-166|tachycardie]], [[Anomalies de couleur des extrémités SD-015|anomalie de couleur des extrémités]], [[État de choc. Main aetiologies: hypovolaemic, septic (see item 158), cardiogenic, anaphylactic|shock]])''''

- Abdominal palpation: pain, lateralized tenderness / tenderness or contracture '''([[Localized or generalized muscle contracture SD-029|localized or generalized muscle contracture]])''''

- Speculum : endo uterine origin of metrorrhagia '''([[Genital bleeding during pregnancy SD-111|genital bleeding during pregnancy]], [[Abnormal genital bleeding (outside known pregnancy) SD-112|abnormal genital bleeding outside known pregnancy,]] [[Acute haemorrhage SD-060|acute haemorrhage]])''''

- Vaginal touch :

o Uterus "too small" for term

o Palpation of an adnexal mass '''([[Pelvic mass SD-106|pelvic mass]])''''

o Sharp pain on palpation of the posterior vaginal pouch (Douglas)


Differential diagnoses '''([[Reasoned request/prescription and choice of a diagnostic test SD-178|reasoned request/prescription and choice of a diagnostic test]]):''''

- If the BHCG is positive:

o Intrauterine pregnancy

o Spontaneous miscarriage

o Hydatidiform mole

- If BHCG is negative:

o Haemorrhagic corpus luteum cyst

o Genital bleeding (not of endo-uterine origin)

o Upper genital infection

o Dinghy twisting

o Non-gynaecological causes: urological (renal colic / UTI +++) / digestive (appendicitis ++)
