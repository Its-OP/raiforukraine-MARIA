Differential diagnosis of head trauma caused by shaking

- accidental cranial trauma'': known, compatible and unchanging circumstances at the time of the interviews, involving sufficient kinetics (fall of more than 1.5 m), different radiological semiology

- haemostasis disorders

- childbirth (suggestive circumstances such as vaginal delivery with dystocia, lesions disappearing in the month following birth, different radiological findings)

- genetic/metabolic diseases (Menkes, organic aciduria) associated with other suggestive clinical signs
