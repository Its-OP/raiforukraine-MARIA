Maternal and child protection is a departmental health service.

The PMI service organises consultations and medical and social prevention and monitoring activities for pregnant women, parents and children under 6, as well as family planning and family education activities.

The service is multidisciplinary (medical, psychological and social), coordinated by a doctor. Nursery nurses and midwives play an important role.

This department is also responsible for applications for approval of childminders, carrying out training activities, monitoring and checking childminders and childcare establishments and services for children under 6.

It is also involved in preventing and dealing with children at risk.

It plays an important role in prevention (vaccination, domestic accidents, attachment disorders, school health in nursery school).

It also plays an important role in supporting children with disabilities.

It also has a public health and epidemiological surveillance role, collecting medical certificates for the 8th day, 9th month and 24th month.
