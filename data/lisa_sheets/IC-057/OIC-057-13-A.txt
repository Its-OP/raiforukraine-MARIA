In the presence of Silverman syndrome, it is important to look for associated lesions (central neurological lesions, mucocutaneous lesions in relation to fractures) as well as other signs of abuse (cf [[Abuse and children at risk: clinical investigation 2C-057-DP-A07]] ).

- physical: looking for mucocutaneous lesions, intracranial lesions inflicted by shaking, retinal haemorrhage secondary to shaking, etc.

- psychological: psychomotor regression, neurodevelopmental disorders

- sexual

- poisoning
