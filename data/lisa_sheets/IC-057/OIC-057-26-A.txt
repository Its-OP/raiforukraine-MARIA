Referral is based on an assessment of the immediate danger, the risks incurred at home, the need for urgent care, the medico-legal urgency and the parents'/carers' willingness to accept care.

- In cases of serious ill-treatment, where there is no possibility of protection, or where a forensic examination is required, the child must always be admitted to hospital. The doctor should report the case as a matter of urgency if the parents refuse or if an urgent forensic examination is required, particularly in cases of sexual assault. Children should be admitted to hospital on a broad basis if maltreatment is suspected.

- In the absence of the above criteria: specialist advice, IP (see Figure)
