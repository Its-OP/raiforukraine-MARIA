'''''Indications for chest radiography in acute community-acquired pneumonia (CAP):'''''

*''Initially,''' for diagnosis because it :
**participates in the positive diagnosis of CAP;
**assesses initial complications (pleural effusion, abscess, obstruction);
*During the reassessment of a CAP which has not progressed favourably under treatment:''''
**look for complications: pleurisy, abscess, etc.
**assess the extent of lesions (sign of severity).


'''''Indications for chest radiography in bronchiolitis (in infants):'''''

A chest X-ray is not necessary for the diagnosis of bronchiolitis.

It can be performed in front of :

- signs of "clinical severity" (requiring hospitalisation);

- suspected differential diagnosis (myocarditis);

- persistence of symptoms after 5 to 7 days
