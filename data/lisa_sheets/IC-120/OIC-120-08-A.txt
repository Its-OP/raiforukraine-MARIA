- Risk factors for immobilisation (e.g. coma, extensive neurological deficit, etc.)

- Risk factors for impairment of general condition and undernutrition

- Risk factors for damage to the skin barrier (e.g. urinary or faecal incontinence leading to skin maceration/infection, etc.)

- Risk factors for impaired tissue perfusion (e.g. chronic arterial disease, acute circulatory failure, etc.)

- Risk factors for superinfection (e.g. diabetes, etc.)
