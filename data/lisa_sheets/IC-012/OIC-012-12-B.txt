''Emergency treatment'': to be assessed on a case-by-case basis depending on the clinical situation

Risk of pregnancy to be assessed on a case-by-case basis, emergency contraception available up to 120 hours after the event<ref>https://www.has-sante.fr/upload/docs/application/pdf/2013-04/fiche-contraception-urgence.pdf</ref> :

*Levonorgestrel (LNG) orally (single dose of 1.5 mg) for up to 72 hours;
*Ulipristal acetate (UPA) orally (single dose of 30 mg) for up to 120 hours;

-Risk of transmission of STIs, [[Know the infectious agents at risk of transmission during an EALB 2C-366-DE-A02|hepatitis and HIV]] :

*No preventive treatment for STIs;
*2C-366-PC-A03|Indications of sero-vaccination after potential exposure to HBV]] ;
*[[Name the main indications that should lead to post-exposure prophylaxis (PEP) for HIV exposure risk 2C-366-PC-B01|Indications for post-exposure prophylaxis (PEP) after potential exposure to HIV]].
