Cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) characteristics in subarachnoid haemorrhage (SAH) ("''[[Cerebrospinal fluid (lcs) analysis SD-183|Cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) analysis]''') :

- macroscopic appearance: hypertensive (high CSF pressure), uniformly red (pinkish or citrine yellow if old SAH), incoagulable, xanthochromic supernatant after centrifugation

- biochemistry: hyperproteinorachia, normoglycorachia, positive for biliary pigments (from the 12th hour)

- cytology: red blood cell/white blood cell ratio > that of plasma, presence of siderophages, altered red blood cells
