The long-term consequences of addictive behaviour during pregnancy are linked to :

- the environment: the mother-child and parent-child bond, family lifestyle, maternal psychological disorders, etc;

- in utero" exposure to alcohol: malformations, psychomotor developmental abnormalities SD-115|mental retardation, learning difficulties, social behaviour disorders;

- possible after-effects of prematurity or foetal suffering "in utero".

This chapter concludes with two key points:

- the importance of an environment of caring professionals ;

- the need for multidisciplinary work.

These are essential guarantees of support for expectant mothers and their newborn babies.
