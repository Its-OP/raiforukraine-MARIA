'''The symptoms of any pancreatic tumour'''

General but late:

- Impaired general condition (decrease in daily activity score), asthenia, anorexia

- Weight loss (often significant at diagnosis)

- Imbalance of a known diabetes

- Deep vein thrombosis (which requires a search for cancer in adults aged over 50)

Linked to the location of the tumour :

- Head of the pancreas :

o Jaundice, often without fever, with discolouration of the stools; may be preceded by the appearance of pruritus, Large palpable gallbladder

o More rarely, signs of upper digestive obstruction due to duodenal compression or invasion.

- Body and tail of the pancreas :

o Epigastric, transfixing, sleepless, severe pain requiring rapid treatment with level 3 analgesics (morphine)

o Back pain, often investigated as dorsalgia

o Late symptoms such as occlusive syndrome of the small intestine due to peritoneal invasion (carcinosis)

<br />Specific symptoms of a secretory pancreatic neuroendocrine tumour (Gastrin: Zollinger-Ellison syndrome associating diarrhoea and multiple duodenal ulcers; Insulin: severe hypoglycaemic malaise; VIP responsible for secretory diarrhoea ....).
