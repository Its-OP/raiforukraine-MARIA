|Title=Know how to diagnose the main complications of herpes zoster (keratitis, post-herpetic pain, neurological complications).

|Contributors=
|Order=9}}
Keratitis: complication of ophthalmic shingles (V1 territory, trigeminal, external nasal branch), this is a diagnostic emergency, the lesion appears 2 to 3 weeks after shingles, ophthalmic pain and corneal lesion, by loss of sensitivity and erosive lesion, sometimes diplopia. Diagnosis is made by fluorescein test; if negative, repeat with slit lamp, epithelial pseudodendrites without terminal bulb.

Post-herpetic pain: neuropathic pain. Persists after healing, generally disappearing after 1 to 6 months, but may be permanent.

Neurological: myelitis and encephalitis sometimes without rash. Oculomotor involvement.
