Clinical form of recurrence: paresthesia-like pain (dysesthesia and disturbance of sensitivity) febrile and eruption in the form of a cluster of small pink vesicles then giving way to an erosion then a crust which heals in 10 days giving way to an indelible depigmented scar, the lesion is generally unilateral, mono metameric (territory of a nerve root). Neuropathic pain may persist, especially in elderly patients.
