- '''Bacterial etiologies:''' ''Shigella, Campylobacter, Salmonella, E. coli'' enteropathogens

- '''Parasitic etiologies:''''

- Malaria can present as febrile gastroenteritis.

- Intestinal amoebosis (amoebic dysentery), particularly if the patient has travelled to Africa, the Middle East, Central America, India or South-East Asia.

- Cryptosporidiosis (cosmopolitan) or giardiosis.

- Intestinal helminthoses (e.g. anguillulosis, tapeworms, ascariasis): may be responsible for transient episodes of diarrhoea alternating with episodes of constipation, bloating and abdominal pain.
