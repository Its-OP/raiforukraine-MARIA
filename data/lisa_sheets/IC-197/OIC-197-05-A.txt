'''. Pelvic spine syndrome ([[Spinal pain (cervical, dorsal or lumbar) SDD-072|72. Spinal pain (cervical, dorsal or lumbar)]]'''

- Inflammatory back pain

- Inflammatory pygalgia (=fessalgia): Sacroiliitis results in the appearance of inflammatory pain in the buttocks. Gluteal pain may be unilateral, bilateral or tilted.

'''. Peripheral joint syndrome ([[Localised or diffuse oedema SDD-054|54. Localised or diffuse oedema]]) ([[56. Joint stiffness]]) ([[67. Joint pain]]) ([[68. Lameness]])'''''

Oligoarthritis of the lower limbs, often affecting large joints.

Coxofemoral involvement, known as coxitis, is seen in severe forms.

'''. Peripheral enthesopathic disease''''

- Enthesitis: the most common and most characteristic is talalgia, which is inflammatory in nature,

- Sausage toe or finger (dactylitis),

- Involvement of the anterior chest wall. '''([[161. Chest pain]])''''
