In France, infectious disease surveillance is mainly carried out by :

*Learn about the 4 main missions of Santé Publique France in the field of infectious diseases 2C-145-DE-B05|Santé Publique France]]: a national public health agency under the supervision of the Ministry of Health. It is a centre of reference and expertise in public health which relies on the National Public Health Network (RNSP) and the [[Learn about the 4 main missions of Santé Publique France in terms of infectious diseases 2C-145-DE-B05|Cellules InterRégionales d'Epidémiologie (CIRE)]].
*The Regional Health Agencies (ARS), in particular through the system of compulsory declaration of diseases (notifiable diseases).
*2C-145-DE-B06|National Reference Centres (CNR): laboratories and clinical departments (located within public or private health, teaching or research establishments), one of whose missions is epidemiological surveillance.
*National surveillance networks: [[Definition of a "sentinel" network 2C-145-DE-B07|réseaux Sentinelles]], RéPIAS ([[Quality and safety of care. Patient safety. Risk management.
