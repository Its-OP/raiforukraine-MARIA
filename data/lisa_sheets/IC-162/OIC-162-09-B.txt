'''Venereal origin:''''

- Gonorrhoea: acute anterior ++, purulent urethral discharge (90% of cases)

- Chlamydia: urethritis (less noisy than for gonococcus) +/- urethral discharge present (< 50% of cases), usually clear, moderate and intermittent.

- Trichomoniasis: subacute urethritis, morning discharge, +/- pruritus


'''Non-venereal origin:'''

- Infectious: Escherichia, Klebsiella, Enterobacter, Serratia, etc.

- Traumatic

- Radique

- Tumoral

- Irritating
