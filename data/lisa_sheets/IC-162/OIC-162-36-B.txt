Clinical signs do not change with age, except in newborns, where the eyes and respiratory tract may be affected.

There is no age-related STI

o However, induced HPV cancers take several years to develop after the first sexual intercourse.

o An STI in a child may raise suspicions of sexual abuse. The questioning and clinical examination must be meticulous and cautious in order not to falsely accuse a parent.

We are currently seeing an increase in the incidence of PID between the ages of 35 and 45, probably linked to the recomposition of couples and the resumption of sexual activity.
