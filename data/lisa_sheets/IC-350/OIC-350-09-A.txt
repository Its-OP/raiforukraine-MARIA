Serious signs to suggest necrotising fasciitis :

- Contributing factors (advanced age, diabetes, OSA, immunodepression);

- Very intense spontaneous pain;

- indurated oedema ;

- Rapid spread of lesions ;

- Hypoesthesia or anaesthesia ;

- Areas of lividity ;

- Haemorrhagic bullae ;

- Crepitus on palpation ;

- Deep necroses (cyanic "geography map" spots).


→ '''Vital emergencies''' which require very rapid medical-surgical management in an intensive care unit.
