* One cigarette = 7,000 compounds, 69 of which are carcinogenic.
* Nicotine is not considered to be carcinogenic.
* Nicotine is the main agent responsible for addiction.
* Tars (carcinogenic) cause epithelial cell changes (but will be replaced by healthy cells if you stop).
* Free radicals from smoke stimulate degradation of the alveolar wall and inhibit protective enzymes.
* Carbon monoxide (CO) causes cardiovascular problems.
