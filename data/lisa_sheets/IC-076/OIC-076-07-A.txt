Signs which appear systematically in certain patients when the blood alcohol level decreases or is zero. Not obligatory in the dependence syndrome (at least 1/3 of dependent patients have no signs of withdrawal).

'''Uncomplicated withdrawal syndrome'''

Symptoms in the hours following cessation of consumption, typically in the morning on waking in highly dependent subjects.

Maximum in the first 72 hours, up to 7-10 days after stopping drinking.

*+++ [[Tremor SD-128|tremor]], [[Hypersudation SD-025|profound sweating]], [[Anxiety SD-116|anxiety]] and [[Vomiting SD-013|vomiting]].
*Other signs:
**[[Sleep disorders, insomnia or hypersomnia SD-135|insomnia]], nightmares, irritability, [[Agitation SD-114|agitation]] ;
**tachycardia, high blood pressure;
**nausea, anorexia, diarrhoea.

Disappear or diminish with alcohol or benzodiazepines.


'''Withdrawal accidents'''

[[Convulsions SD-120|seizures]] or delirium tremens (significant risk of death).

'''Convulsive withdrawal seizures'''

Within 48 hours of stopping or significantly reducing alcohol consumption (rarely later)

Tonic-clonic generalised seizures

Risk of rapid recurrence, malignant state, progression to delirium tremens.   


'''Delirium tremens''': state of [[Mental confusion/disorientation SD-119|confusion]] agitated and delirious.

*agitation,
*major tremors,
*incoherent speech with dreamlike delirium and intense delusional experience,
*Hallucinations SD-122|hallucinations]] multi-sensorial, especially visual (zoopsies); or experience of a scene of aggression (fear reactions: risk of running away or aggression).
*moderate fever, profuse sweating, changes in blood pressure, tachycardia.

Risk of dehydration, epileptic seizures and inhalation pneumonitis.
