The diagnosis is most often made in young adults. The usual circumstances for diagnosis are as follows:

*During a check-up for hypertension in adults and children|HTA

*Revealed by a renal complication:
**lumbar pain or heaviness
**macroscopic hematuria
**lithiasis and calculi 2C-265-DE-A01|nephritic colic
**infection of a renal cyst
**[[Chronic renal failure in adults and children|renal failure]]

*The discovery of renal and/or hepatic cysts on ultrasound.

*During a family investigation

*On the occasion of an extrarenal event (e.g. stroke, arterial aneurysm rupture or complication of a hepatic cyst).
