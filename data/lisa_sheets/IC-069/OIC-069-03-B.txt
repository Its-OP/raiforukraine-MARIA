|Title=Knowing the risk factors predisposing to psychological disorders in pregnancy and the post-partum period
|Description=Risk factors linked to psychiatric vulnerability, gynaecological or obstetric factors, environmental factors, etc.
|Rubric=Epidemiology
|Contributors=El-Hage Wissam
|Order=3}}

* Psychiatric vulnerability: psychiatric or family history of [[Sexual violence SD-350|personal]], substance use disorder.
* Gynaecological or obstetric factors: age <20 and >35, primiparity, unwanted pregnancy, foetal malformation or pathology, gestational diabetes, pregnancy-induced hypertension, complicated delivery, prematurity, hypotrophy.
* Environmental factors: isolation, relationship difficulties, economic insecurity, low level of education, history of [[Psychological and/or physical violence SD-351|trauma]], migration.
