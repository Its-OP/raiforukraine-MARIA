|'''Sulfamethoxazole and trimethoprim''''
|Documented antibiotic therapy for urinary tract infections, otitis and sinusitis, prevention and treatment of pneumocystis, alternative to penicillin A for the treatment of L. monocytogenes infections.
|Infants (first 6 weeks of life), breast-feeding, hypersensitivity to the active substance or to one of the excipients, in cases of G6PD deficiency, severe liver damage, severe renal insufficiency.
|Enterobacteriaceae, L. monocytogenes, staphylococci, Pneumocystis jirovecii.
|Allergies, cytopenias, renal failure


Interactions: methotrexate, phenytoin, hyperkalemic drugs, repaglinide, letformin, paclitaxel, ciclosporin, antivitamins K
