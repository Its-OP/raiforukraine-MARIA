Premenstrual syndrome (PMS) is the functional discomfort most frequently reported by women (30 to 40%). It is a group of benign symptoms that can affect a number of different systems, the only thing they have in common being that they are cyclical, appearing in the days before the period and disappearing at the start of or during the period.

Classically, there are three main signs:

*breast clinical examination abnormality SD-164|breast clinical examination abnormality]'': isolated mastodynia with clinical examination of the breasts, which can sometimes be really painful and tense;
*abdomino-pelvic signs: more or less pronounced abdominal bloating, corresponding to a phenomenon of pelvic congestion, always annoying, often associated with a transit disorder with sometimes even a transient ''[[Weight gain SD-057|weight gain]]''' of 3 to 4 kg maximum;
*neuro-psychic signs: irritability, anxiety and depressive syndrome, usually of moderate intensity; sometimes even headaches. It should be noted that there is a psychiatric form of premenstrual syndrome called premenstrual dysphoric syndrome included in the classification of mental disorders (DSM-IV). It corresponds to the occurrence of disabling cyclical psychiatric signs or syndromes, which affect women's quality of life and well-being.
