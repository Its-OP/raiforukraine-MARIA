Bleeding is quantified by :

*questioning: number of pads, clots, clothing stained by bleeding, etc. ;
*Higham score for menorrhagia;
*speculum examination: visualisation of active bleeding;
*assessment of blood spoliation: HemoCue®, CBC (haemoglobin level). '''([[Anomaly of erythrocyte indices (haemoglobin level, haematocrit...) SD-214|Anomaly of erythrocyte indices (haemoglobin level, haematocrit...]]), [[Haemoglobin decrease SD-217|haemoglobin decrease]])'''''


Clinical assessment of the impact is carried out by looking for :

*signs of poorly tolerated anaemia:
**'''[[Tachycardia SD-166|Tachycardia]]'''''
**Mucocutaneous and conjunctival pallor
**'''[[Dyspnée SD-162|Dyspnée]]'''
**'''[[Asthenia SD-021|Asthenia]]''''
**Vertigo ('''[[Vertigo and dizziness SD-064|vertigo and dizziness]]''')
*signs of shock:
**Hypotension
**Malaise (''[[Malaise/loss of consciousness SD-050|malaise/loss of consciousness]]'')
**Sweating
**Cold extremities
**Marbling.
