Etiology of metrorrhagia ([[Abnormal genital bleeding (outside known pregnancy) SD-112|'''abnormal genital bleeding (outside known pregnancy)''']]), classified according to different modalities.


(In women of childbearing age, metrorrhagia should be investigated for pregnancy (''[[Genital bleeding during pregnancy SD-111|genital bleeding during pregnancy]]'').


{| class="wikitable"
|'''Uterine and adnexal organic causes'''
|<nowiki>- Adenomyosis (</nowiki>'''[[Pelvic pain SD-099|pelvic pain]]''''')

- Uterine myoma (mainly submucosal) ('''[[Pelvic mass SD-106|pelvic mass]]''')

- Endometrial polyp

- Endometrial hyperplasia

- Endometrial cancer

- Upper genital infection (''''[[Hyperthermia/fever SD-044|hyperthermia/fever]], [[Pelvic pain SD-099|pelvic pain]], [[Leucorrhoea SD-104|leucorrhoea]]''''')

- Secretory tumours of the ovary

- Arteriovenous malformations

|'''Functional causes''''
|Once all organic aetiologies have been ruled out, the liver may become dysfunctional:

- Intermenstrual bleeding on the 14th day of the cycle (ovulation)

- Pre-menstrual metrorrhagia due to luteal insufficiency

- Post-menstrual metrorrhagia due to oetrogen deficiency at the beginning of the cycle
