|Title=Knowing the indications of an electrocardiogram (ECG)
|Description=The main indications for cardioscopes and resting ECGs with 12-15 or 18 leads. Be familiar with the main emergency indications: organ failure(s), chest pain, palpitations, syncope, hydroelectrolytic disorders (K+, Ca) and know how to perform a 12-lead ECG.

An ECG is indicated:

*In the event of ''symptoms'': dyspnoea, palpitations, chest pain, malaise/syncope, etc.
*Initial assessment or follow-up of cardiac or non-cardiac pathologies that may give rise to cardiovascular complications (e.g. diabetes)
*HTA check-up ''''HTA'''
*Family investigation'' in the case of certain hereditary heart diseases
*''Pre-operative assessment'' depending on the patient's condition and the type of surgery required
*Professional aptitude'' (drivers, pilots) or ''sporting aptitude'' (high-risk or competitive sports).
