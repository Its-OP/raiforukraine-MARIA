'''Hypokalemia''' :

* Flat or diffusely negative T waves under ST segment shift,
* QT prolongation +/- appearance of a U wave,
* Hyperexcitability with extrasystoles, atrial or ventricular arrhythmia, torsades de pointes.

Hyperkalaemia:

* Broad, sharp, symmetrical T waves,
* Conduction disorders with prolongation of PR, widening of QRS,
* Risk of BAV, sinus dysfunction.
