* Early stage I :
** ST segment elevation concave upwards, without mirror, without Q wave.
** Positive T waves,
** Diffuse PQ sub-shift,

* Stage II:
** Flat T waves,
**isoelectric T waves,

* Stage III: Negative T waves,

* Stage IV (< 1 month): Progressive normalisation,

* Electrical alternation in case of tamponade

* QRS ''microvoltage'' (QRS amplitude <5mV in peripheral leads).

* ''Supraventricular rhythm disorders are frequent'' in cases of pericarditis
