Clinical picture characterised by:

- Hyper-eosinophilia and/or lymphocytosis with mononucleosis syndrome;

- non-specific ''cutaneous eruption'' which may take the form of maculopapular exanthema or erythroderma; ''infiltrated'' (oedema of the face and extremities);

- diffuse adenopathy ;

- high fever ;

- life-threatening visceral damage (hepatitis and interstitial nephropathy; interstitial lung disease, myocarditis, macrophagic activation syndrome, etc.):

The usual time between the start of inducing treatment and the appearance of the first signs: 2 to 6 weeks.
