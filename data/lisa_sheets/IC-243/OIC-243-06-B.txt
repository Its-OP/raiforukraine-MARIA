'''Specific biological signs'''

1/ An increase in TSH (peripheral hypothyroidism)

2/ a decrease in T4L with normal or low TSH (unsuitable for the low T4L value) (central hypothyroidism)


'''Non-specific biological signs'''

1. haematological abnormalities

- normocytic and normochromic anaemia, more rarely macrocytic

- coagulation disorders and haemostasis factor abnormalities

'''2. Ionic and metabolic abnormalities''''

*elevated LDL cholesterol;
*hypertriglyceridaemia (VLDL)
*an increase in muscle enzymes (CPK), sometimes with an increase in ASAT and LDH;
*dilution hyponatremia in myxedematous coma.
