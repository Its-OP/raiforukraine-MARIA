'''Bilateral and comparative standard X-rays''' of all affected joints: without delaying joint puncture, to look for (i) arguments for a positive diagnosis, (ii) the absence of elements for differential diagnoses and (iii) as an initial work-up for subsequent follow-up.

'''Ultrasound:''' to confirm the existence of a joint effusion in cases of doubt (obese patients, etc.), eliminate differential diagnoses and guide puncture in the case of a deep joint or small effusion.
