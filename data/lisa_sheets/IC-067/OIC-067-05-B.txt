== In young children
First signs of hyperactivity/impulsivity often from the age of walking, but symptoms are not very stable before the age of 4.

== School-age children ==

* Inattention is often identified when attentional demands increase (primary schools, secondary school).
* Diagnosis may be delayed if attention disorders SD-129|attention disorders]] are predominant.

== Adolescents and adults ==

* Motor hyperactivity/agitation tends to decrease with age.
* Impulsivity and inattention remain problematic for 2/3 of people.
* Co-occurring disorders (depression, substance abuse disorder), complications and social and educational/occupational repercussions may be at the forefront, with a risk of misdiagnosis.


NB: in girls, inattention is often predominant and diagnosis delayed.
