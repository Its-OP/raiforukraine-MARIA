The characteristics of a disease justifying the introduction of screening are as follows:

- The screening programme must meet an identified need,

- The objectives of the screening must be defined in advance,

- The target population must be defined,

- The effectiveness of the screening programme must be scientifically proven,

- It must include education, the test, what to do afterwards and how to organise it,

- There must be quality assurance, with techniques to reduce the risks associated with screening,

- The programme must allow for an informed decision, confidentiality and respect for autonomy,

- The programme must promote equity and access to the programme for the entire target population,

- The evaluation of this programme must be planned in advance,

- The benefit-risk balance must be favourable.
