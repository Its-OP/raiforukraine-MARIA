'''CT signs of uncomplicated diverticulitis are:'''

-thickening of the colon wall,

-infiltration of fat around one or more diverticula.


'''Signs of complications are:'''

-for abscesses: a liquid or aeriform and liquid collection, the size of which must be specified,

-for peritonitis: the existence of pneumoperitoneum bubbles (extra-colic air) and/or fluid and/or faeces in the peritoneal cavity,

-sigmoido-vesical fistula: the presence of gas in the urinary tract.


The Hinchey classification is used to assess the severity of complicated diverticulitis (clinico-radiological):

Stage I: phlegmon or pericolic abscess,

Stage II: pelvic, abdominal or retroperitoneal abscess (localised peritonitis),

Stage III: Purulent generalised peritonitis,

Stage IV: faecal peritonitis
