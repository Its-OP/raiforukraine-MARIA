'''1. unconjugated bilirubin icterus'''

Clear urine or "coca cola" in the case of haemolysis

Normal stools

Haemolytic jaundice: increase in reticulocytes (with or without anaemia), LDH, decrease in haptoglobin.

'''2.   Conjugated bilirubin icterus'''

Dark urine

Discoloured stools

Pruritus (particularly in cases of primary biliary cholangitis)

Large gallbladder (tumour obstruction of the main bile duct below the junction of the cyt duct)

Typical biliary pain if lithiasis of the main bile duct

Biological cholestasis: increase in alkaline phosphatase and GGT

If cholestasis is prolonged:

- Deficiencies in fat-soluble vitamins A, D, E and K

- Low PT with normal factor V

- Steatorrhea

- Osteopenia

- Fibrosis/ Secondary biliary cirrhosis (exceptional)

Anti-mitochondrial antibodies (in cases of primary biliary cholangitis)
