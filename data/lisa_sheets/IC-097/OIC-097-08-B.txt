'''Specific treatments'''

- Treatment should begin early, at best within the first two weeks.

- Two methods are possible '''([[Prescribing and monitoring a vascular approach SD-271|prescribing and monitoring a vascular approach]])''' :

- Polyvalent immunoglobulins

- Plasma exchanges

- These treatments mean less time on assisted ventilation, an earlier return to walking and shorter hospital stays.

- Corticosteroids are unnecessary.

'''Symptomatic treatments'''

- Essential prevention of decubitus complications '''([[Management of a patient in prolonged decubitus SD-276|management of a patient in prolonged decubitus]]):''' heparinotherapy with low molecular weight heparin, prevention of vicious positions favoured by deficits.

- Mechanical ventilation if necessary,

- Controlling dysautonomia,

- Early rehabilitation,

- Social measures (return to work).
