The vaccination calendar is updated every year and is available on the website of the Ministry of Health and Solidarity (https://solidarites-sante.gouv.fr/prevention-en-sante/preserver-sa-sante/vaccination/calendrier-vaccinal).

In infants: vaccinations are compulsory before the age of 2 if born on or after 1 January 2018:

*2 months: hexavalent (diphtheria, tetanus, poliomyelitis, whooping cough, Haemophilus influenzae b, hepatitis B) and pneumococcus
*4 months: hexavalent and pneumococcus
*5 months invasive meningococcal infection
*11 months hexavalent and pneumococcus
*12 months 2<sup>e</sup> meningococcal and primary measles, mumps and rubella (MMR) vaccination
*16 to 18 months 2<sup>e</sup> MMR

The meningococcal C vaccine is recommended for children born after 2018 between the ages of 12 months and 24 years.

The following vaccines are also recommended:

*rotavirus: vaccination by oral vaccine before the age of 6 months (2 or 3 doses depending on the vaccine used, starting at 2 months)
*meningococcal B: 3 doses at 3, 5 and 12 months of age


'''In children''' :

*If at risk of severe flu: annual flu vaccine flu vaccine offered to all children aged 2 to 17 (intra-nasal vaccine).
*Between the ages of 6 and 8: 1 booster for diphtheria, tetanus, poliomyelitis and whooping cough (DTPC).
*Between the ages of 11 and 13, 2<sup>th</sup> booster dose of dTPca is compulsory.
*HPV vaccine recommended for boys and girls aged 11 to 14 (possible from age 9), up to the age of 19 (26 for men who have sex with men).
*Hepatitis B catch-up recommended


'''In adults:'''

*If there is a risk of severe flu: annual flu vaccine
*dTPca: age 25 recommended
*dTP: 45, 65, 75 and 85 years recommended
*From age 65, see next paragraph
