|Title=Knowing the clinical signs suggestive of obstructive sleep apnoea syndrome (SAS) in adults
|Description=None
|Rubric=Positive diagnosis
|Contributors=Iulia Ioan,Laurianne Coutier
|Order=17}}

- Excessive daytime sleepiness

- Severe daily snoring

- Breathing pauses observed by the spouse

- Sensations of choking or suffocation during sleep

- Repeated awakenings during sleep

- Nocturia

- Night sweats

- Unrefreshing sleep

- Daytime fatigue

- Morning headache

- Difficulties with concentration and memory

- Mood disorders

- Decreased libido
