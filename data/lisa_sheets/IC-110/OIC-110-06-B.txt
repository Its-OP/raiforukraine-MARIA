Exploring the disorder to characterise it. Look for organic causes in the history and clinical examination. Carry out a paraclinical assessment in the event of an anomaly suggestive of an organic disorder (polysomnography, ENT examination).

In children: Above all, a preventive approach. If there is an organic cause, treat the cause.

Sleep diary and exploration of family habits to encourage behaviours that improve the quality of sleep. Explore the sleep environment, evening activities, bedtime rituals and intra-family relationships.

Refer parents to a 2nd<sup>e</sup> or 3rd<sup>e</sup> recourse consultation in the event of organic or psychiatric pathology.

Rarely drug treatment (sleeping pills, anti-histamines, even melatonin) and only as a 2nd or 3rd resort.
