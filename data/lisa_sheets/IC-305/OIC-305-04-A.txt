'''In the asymptomatic period, oeso-gastro-duodenal endoscopy'' (EOGD)

- OBC monitoring every 2 to 5 years, depending on its extent.

- Screening for alcoholics and smokers enables diagnosis at this time


'''At the symptomatic period''':

- Progressive ''dysphagia'' with solids and then liquids (sensation of food stopping in the oesophagus for a short time)

- Associated signs :

o Weight loss (often > 5 to 10% of body weight)

o More rarely :

§ Dysphonia due to invasion of the recurrent nerve.

§ Coughing after swallowing (sign of an oesotracheal or oesobronchial fistula)

§ Chest or back pain

§ Palpation of a left supraclavicular lymph node (Troisier)

§ Digestive haemorrhage (haematemesis and/or melena)

§ Symptoms associated with metastases
