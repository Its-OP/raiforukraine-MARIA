'''Patient-related factors''':

- Altemeier classification: contamination class according to the degree of contamination of the wound preoperatively; takes the values 1 (clean), 2 (clean-contaminated), 3 (contaminated), 4 (dirty).

- ASA score: risk scale developed by the American Society for Anesthesiology, which takes into account the preoperative pathological state of patients, with values of 1 (no condition other than that requiring surgery), 2 (moderate disturbance of a major function), 3 (severe disturbance of a major function), 4 (imminent vital risk), 5 (moribund).

- Obesity, diabetes

- Malnutrition

- Age

- Comorbidities (organ failure, metabolic factors, etc.)

'''Intervention-related factors''' :

- Prolonged operating time beyond the usual standards

- Failure to maintain intraoperative normothermia

- Tissue oxygenation

- Haemostasis

- Controlling blood sugar levels

'''Factors related to prevention and non-compliance with preoperative protocols'' :

- Skin preparation and disinfection

- Antibiotic prophylaxis
