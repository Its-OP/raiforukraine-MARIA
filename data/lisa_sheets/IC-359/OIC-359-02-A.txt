- Acute respiratory distress (inspection diagnosis):

o Dyspnoea

o Tachypnoea (N.B: normal respiratory rate varies according to age*. Apnoeas can be a sign of respiratory distress in young infants)

o Signs of struggle: pulling, thoraco-abdominal rocking (+ xiphoid funneling, expiratory whining and nasal flapping in infants)

o Signs of hypoxaemia (cyanosis, SpO<sub>2</sub>) and/or hypercapnia (sweating, asterixis, hypertension, hypovigilance).

- Acute respiratory failure: hypoxaemia and/or hypercapnia (excluding metabolic alkalosis)
