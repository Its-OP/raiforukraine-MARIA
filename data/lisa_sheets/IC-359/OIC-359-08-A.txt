- Anamnesis: history, mode of onset, accompanying signs (cough, sputum, chest pain, orthopnea, fever, etc.), notion of penetration syndrome, etc.

- Abnormalities on respiratory examination :

o Characteristics (e.g. percussion: dullness/tympany; auscultation: crackles, tubal murmur, sibilants, etc.)

o Symmetrical or asymmetrical

- Extra-respiratory signs: fever, signs of right heart failure (IMO, jugular turgor, hepato-jugular reflux)
