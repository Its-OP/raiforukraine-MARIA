*Typically, the patient presents to emergency with [[2C-269|suprapubic pain 269]] and an inability to urinate despite bladder repletion, sometimes with agitation.

*<Interview: urological history, associated symptoms (fever, haematuria, transit disorders), symptoms preceding retention (dysuria, sensation of poor bladder emptying), usual treatments.

*<Clinical examination: presence of a bladder globe characterised by a hypogastric curve and suprapubic dullness. A rectal examination in men is necessary to make an aetiological diagnosis (faecal impaction, BPH, prostatitis, prostate cancer) and pelvic examinations in women (prolapse, pelvic tumour, faecal impaction).

*False [[2C-125|urinary incontinence 125]] must be ruled out; in this case, it is overflow incontinence.
