</gallery>'''Abscess''': Localized accumulation of pus in a cavity by the development of infection and whose walls are made of the neighbouring tissue modified and repressed.

'''Panaris''': acute infection of the soft parts of a finger, which may be located periungual, in the pulpal area or on the dorsal surface of a phalanx.

N.B.: Anthracoid panicitis is superficial, centred on a hair on the back of a phalanx.

Sheath phlegmon'': infection of the sheath space in which the flexor tendons of the finger and hand run.
