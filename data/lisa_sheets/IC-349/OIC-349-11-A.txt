</gallery>'''Severity criteria:'''

*Marked general signs (fever, hypotension, tachycardia, desaturation, loss of alertness).
*Local signs: lymphangitis, extensive cellulitis, exposure of bone or tendon structures.
*Rapid evolution kinetics
*Patient at risk

'''Indications for hospitalisation:''''

The following presentations are surgical indications:

*Collected abscess
*Sheath phlegmon
*Bite
*Osteitis or arthritis
*Collected panaris
*Wound with tendon, bone or joint exposure
*necrotising fasciitis (occurs in a noisy picture of severe infection in patients at risk and may require resuscitation)
