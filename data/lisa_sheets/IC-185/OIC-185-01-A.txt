The inflammatory reaction (IR) is the body's defence mechanism and part of the innate immune response. It is triggered when the body is attacked, either exogenously during an infection or trauma, or endogenously and sterilely (uric acid or cholesterol crystals, cell necrosis during ischaemia, for example).
