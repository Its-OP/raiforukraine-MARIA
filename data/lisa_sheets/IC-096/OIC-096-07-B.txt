'''Hereditary and primary amyloidosis (gammopathy)'''

Amyloid polyneuropathy is secondary to deposits of amyloid substance (mutated prealbumin in hereditary amyloidosis or monoclonal gammopathy in primary amyloidosis) in the nerve.

A search for amyloidosis should be carried out when :

- Chronic axonal polyneuropathy ;

- Comportant une expression dysautonomique riche '''; ([[Troubles sexuels et troubles de l'érection SD-063|troubles sexuels et troubles de l'érection]]) ([[Vertige et sensation vertigineuse SD-064|vertige et sensation vertigineuse]]) ([[Malaise/perte de connaissance SD-050|malaise/perte de connaissance]])''''

- And uni- or bilateral carpal tunnel syndrome;

- And systematically in a family context.

Proof is obtained from the presence of amyloid substance in tissue, particularly in the sensory nerve after nerve biopsy ('''[[Interpretation of an anatomopathology report SD-180|interpretation of an anatomopathology report]]'''); from the demonstration of a mutation in the transthyretin (prealbumin) gene in familial forms.  

'''Infectious causes'''

AIDS can lead to sensory polyneuropathy in the late phase of the disease '''([[Discovery positive diagnosis rapid screening vi h SD-235|découverte diagnostic positif dépistage rapide VIH]])'''

'''Other causes'''

Predominant pain, an associated motor deficit and an asymmetric onset of neuropathy should raise the suspicion of advanced vasculitis '''([[Acute or chronic inflammatory syndrome SD-186|acute or chronic inflammatory syndrome]])''''

and discuss a nerve biopsy.
