'''Acute or rapidly progressive glomerulonephritis (GNRP)''' (cf item 261) ''':''''

'''-'''' To be recognised early because of '''therapeutic emergencies'''.

- Typically glomerular syndrome with ''proteinuria'' (> 1 g/g), and abundant ''haematuria'' (> 10<sup>5</sup>/mL) sometimes even macroscopic.

- Urgent PBR, immunological work-up essential. The four types of cause :

* Acute post-infectious glomerulonephritis or chronic infectious conditions: macroscopic haematuria, oedema, hypertension, renal failure (= nephritic syndrome) 10 to 15 days after an episode of ENT or cutaneous infection;
* Pauci-immune necrotising glomerulonephritis with extra-capillary proliferation: anti-cytoplasmic cell antibody (ANCA) vasculitis, microscopic polyangiitis, granulomatosis with polyangiitis, or eosinophilic granulomatosis with polyangiitis;
Glomerulonephritis with immune deposits and endo and extracapillary proliferation complicating lupus, mixed cryoglobulinaemia, rheumatoid purpura, etc.; * Goodyear's disease;
* Goodpasture's disease, where glomerular AKI is often associated with haemoptysis revealing intra-alveolar haemorrhage.
