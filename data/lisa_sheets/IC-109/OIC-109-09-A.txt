Post-fall syndrome includes:

- a ''motor component'' :

- in the armchair, the patient tends to retropelate and is unable to move into antepulsion;

- Standing, the gait is heeling, with a widening of the support polygon and bending of the knees;

- a ''psychological component'': this takes the form of major anxiety; the patient is afraid of the previous emptiness; at its most severe, the condition is known as astasia-abasia;

- psychomotor regression syndrome'': this can complicate the post-fall syndrome with the appearance of a search for dependence (clinophilia, incontinence, help with meals);

- depressive syndrome
