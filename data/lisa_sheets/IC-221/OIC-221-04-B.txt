Atherosclerosis

- is more common in men, but is underestimated in women,

- begins around the age of 50 in men and around 10 years later in women, with its frequency increasing with age,

- progressively worsens over time if left untreated,

- is responsible for 500 cases/100,000 inhabitants in Finland, compared with 125 cases/100,000 inhabitants in South-Eastern Europe, reflecting a North-South gradient.
