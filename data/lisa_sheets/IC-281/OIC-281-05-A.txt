- Epigastric in location, radiating posteriorly (transfixing), it is typically triggered by food or alcohol intake. The pain may be due to a flare-up of acute pancreatitis on PC, or to chronic inflammation.

- The course of the pain varies, from several hours to a few days.

- Secondary weight loss is partly linked to pain, with patients restricting the diet that triggers pain
