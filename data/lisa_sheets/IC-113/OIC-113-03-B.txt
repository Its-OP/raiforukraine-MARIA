===1- Malignant tumours in infants===
A vascularised malignant tumour (rhabdomyosarcoma, infantile fibrosarcoma, etc.) should be suspected in the presence of a congenital, single, hard, progressive tumour => "biopsy at the slightest doubt".

===''2- '' Vascular malformations (see table 1)===
- A superficial haemangioma at the initial stage may simulate a vascular malformation (planar angioma).

- Certain venous or lymphatic malformations can simulate a deep subcutaneous haemangioma (Doppler ultrasound shows rapid flow in haemangiomas and slow flow in venous or lymphatic malformations).

===3- Congenital haemangiomas===
- Different from infantile haemangiomas.

- Present at birth, sometimes detected by antenatal ultrasound.

- variable course: rapid regression or persistence.

- Propranolol ineffective
<br />

=== ''4- Kasabach-Merritt syndrome'' ===
- Tumour intravascular coagulation associated with thrombocytopenia.

- Does not complicate infantile haemangiomas but rare congenital vascular tumours.

- Rapid appearance of a large, ecchymotic and inflammatory swelling.

- Specialist hospital treatment.
