'''1- Positive diagnosis'''

- Arteriovenous malformations" are manifested by one or more red macules simulating a flat angioma, but which are warm, extensive and flapping, evolving into a swelling or localised deformity.

- The diagnosis is based on :

- palpation: thrill ;

- auscultation: murmur ;

- Doppler ultrasound: rapid flow.

- Location: the ears, scalp and extremities of the limbs are the most frequently affected.

- MRI indicated.

- Specialist advice essential


2- '''Evolution and complications'''

- Spontaneous flare-ups or flare-ups triggered by trauma or an attempted therapeutic procedure, puberty or pregnancy.

- Risk of haemorrhage, ischaemic necrosis, locoregional invasion, recurrence after treatment
