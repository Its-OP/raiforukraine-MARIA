'''1- Clinical diagnosis''' ( biopsy and imaging usually unnecessary).

- Swelling of elastic consistency (not hard) without quivering on palpation or murmur on auscultation.

- Variable size, number and location.

'''Three clinical types of haemangioma''':

- ''Superficial haemangioma'' (= a bright red nodule or plaque (figure 113.2), in relief, with a tense or nippled surface, without complete emptying or disappearance on pressure.

- Deep hemangioma (or subcutaneous hemangioma) = bluish swelling or swelling the colour of normal skin (imaging sometimes necessary: Doppler ultrasound shows a richly vascularised tumour with rapid flow).

- Mixed haemangioma'' combines superficial and deep components (figure 113.3).
