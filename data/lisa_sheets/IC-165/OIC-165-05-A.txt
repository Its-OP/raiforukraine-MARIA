Complications of urinary tract infection are mainly related to neurological damage, but also to damage to the testicles, ovaries and pancreas.

Meningitis/Encephalitis'': urlian meningitis may precede parotitis. Neurological signs are reported in around 10% of cases, and the outcome is often favourable.

Other neurological manifestations: paralysis or deafness

'''Orchitis''': testicular pain following enlargement due to inflammation. Rare progression to testicular atrophy (sterility).

Pancreatitis: nausea and vomiting accompanied by sudden epigastric pain
