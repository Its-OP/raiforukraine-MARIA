Nociceptive pain is ''pain associated with activation of peripheral nociceptors by local tissue damage.''


The most frequent clinical situations of acute nociceptive pain include :

- Post-traumatic pain, including post-operative pain

- Pain caused by treatment

- Acute inflammatory pain: arthritis, visceral pain with an inflammatory component (renal colic, gastroenteritis or colitis) or not (intestinal spasm).

'''<u>Particularities of care</u>''''


'''Etiological treatment +++'''

'''Symptomatic treatment:''''

- Anticipate and try to prevent pain: before treatment, mobilisation, etc.

- Systematically provide appropriate treatment

- Patient information and education


'''<u>Medication treatment</u>'''

- Level 1, 2 and 3 analgesics (see corresponding sections)

- MEOPA (see corresponding section)

- General, locoregional and local anaesthesia

- Local anaesthetics

'''<u>Non-drug treatment</u>''''

- Relaxation techniques (hypnoanalgesia, etc.)

- Immobilisation, rehabilitation, physiotherapy as required
