The main risk factors for so-called primary osteoarthritis (occurring in a healthy joint) are (for all joints) :

- Age

- Overweight, obesity and metabolic syndrome

- Heredity / family history

- The female sex

- Excessive mechanical stress (repeated microtrauma, history of meniscus or ligament damage, genu varum, genu valgum, etc.)
