In the event of disruptive behavioural problems (agitation, aggression, aberrant motor behaviour, etc.)

1.     Rule out confusional syndrome+++ (break with previous state? disturbance of alertness?), which represents a diagnostic emergency.

Look for a factor triggering or aggravating the behavioural problems (INFECTION, IATROGENIA, URINARY WITHHOLDING, PAIN, anxiety-depressive syndrome, change in environment, caregivers, etc.).

2 ''Protecting the patient and protecting oneself'' (assessing the patient's self- and hetero-aggressiveness and the dangerousness of the environment)

3.     Give priority to "non-medicinal approaches": calm, soothing words, not contradicting words, occupational activities.

4.     If necessary (if there is a danger to the patient or those around them), medication: give preference to benzodiazepines with a short half-life, or atypical antipsychotics can be used temporarily as part of short courses of treatment (2 to 3 weeks). The side effects of these drugs are numerous and patients receiving them must be monitored.

5.     Reassessment of the appropriateness of the medicinal and non-medicinal approaches used.

6.     Therapeutic education for carers
