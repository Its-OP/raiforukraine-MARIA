Cerebral imaging (MRI at best if no contraindication) should be performed for any neurocognitive disorder of recent discovery (initial diagnosis).

Two objectives

1.     Rule out a curable cause of cognitive impairment (subdural haematoma, normal pressure hydrocephalus, tumour, etc.).

2.     To obtain morphological elements that may point to the aetiology of cognitive disorders

a.     Localised atrophy (T1, hippocampal coronal sections, etc.)

b.     Vascular lesions (FLAIR) Microbleeds (T2*) Recent ischaemia (diffusion)
