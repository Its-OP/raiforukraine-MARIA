'''TYPICAL ULCER SYNDROME:''' it associates epigastric pain with a sensation of cramp or painful hunger calmed by eating without irradiation characterised by its periodicity. This periodicity is twofold: during the day, late postprandial (1 to 3 hours after meals), and during the year with daily symptomatic periods interspersed with periods of calm lasting a few months.


'''ATYPICAL ULCEROUS SYNDROME: frequent,''' right or left hypochondral pain, radiating dorsally, lack of periodicity, hyperalgesic form

Asymptomatic, diagnosed by chance during oeso-gastroduodenal endoscopy (2/3 of cases in some studies).

'''Physical examination generally normal in the absence of complications'''
