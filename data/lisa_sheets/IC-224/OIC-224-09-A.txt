The majority of cases of hypertension in adults are of essential origin, but it can sometimes be secondary to :

- substance abuse (alcohol, drugs such as cocaine, etc.)

- medication (corticoids, anti-inflammatories, oestrogens, nasal vasoconstrictors, etc.)

- chronic renal failure in adults and children|nephropathy

- vascular nephropathy|renal artery stenosis]], whether atheromatous or due to fibromuscular dysplasia

- an endocrine cause: primary hyperaldosteronism, pheochromocytoma, pituitary adenoma, Cushing's syndrome
