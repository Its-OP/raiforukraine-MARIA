Resistant hypertension is defined as uncontrolled hypertension despite [[Atheroma: epidemiology and pathophysiology. The polyatheromatous patient|adapted lifestyle hygiene]] and triple anti-hypertensive therapy at optimal doses comprising a [[Diuretics/know the mechanisms of action, indications, side effects drug interactions, monitoring procedures and main causes of failure 2C-330-PC-A02|thiazide diuretic]] and usually a [[https://sides.uness.fr/lisa/M%C3%A9dicaments_du_syst%C3%A8me_r%C3%A9nine-angiotensine:_connaitre_les_m%C3%A9canismes_d%27action,_indications,_effets_secondaires_interactions_m%C3%A9dicamenteuses,_modalit%C3%A9s_de_surveillance_et_principales_causes_d%27%C3%A9chec_2C-330-PC-A04 ACE inhibitor/ARB2]] and a [[Calcium channel antagonists|calcium channel blocker]].

Resistance factors must be sought:

- Therapeutic education, compliance and self-medication|Therapeutic non-adherence

- [[Drug interactions 2C-322-PC-A12|drug interactions]] or use of hypertensive substances

- Atheroma: epidemiology and pathophysiology. The polyatheromatous patient|excessive sodium intake]]

- [[Alcohol addiction.|excessive alcohol consumption]]

- Sleep disorders in children and adults|obstructive sleep apnoea-hypopnoea syndrome]]
