In France, the estimated prevalence of cirrhosis is in the region of 2,000 to 3,300 cases per million inhabitants (0.2 to 0.3%), with an annual incidence of 150 to 200 cases per million inhabitants.
In all, around 500,000 people in France suffer from advanced hepatic fibrosis (F3 and cirrhosis), and 16,000 people die from the condition every year.
