*Otalgia'' with paroxysms, ''fever'', +/- otorrhea, sometimes not very symptomatic

*Appearance of the eardrum:
**congestive stage: bright pinkish-red eardrum; preservation of the relief of the malleus (no retro-tympanic effusion)
**suppurated stage:
***closed eardrum: purplish-red, bulging eardrum; disappearance of malleus relief; or yellowish (collection of retro-tympanic pus)
***open eardrum (perforated): punctiform perforation, throbbing purulent otorrhoea
**check contralateral tympanum

*Clinical forms:
** infant: bilateral, altered general condition; sometimes hyperthermia, weight loss, hypothrepsia, gastroenteritis.
** adult: rarer, sometimes torpid and asthenic in elderly/diabetic patients
** phlyctenular: very painful, otorrhagia, haemorrhagic tympanic phlycthenes; retrotympanic effusion
** barotraumatic: serous or serohaemorrhagic effusion - diving accident with tubal dyspermeability; severe otalgia, blocked ear sensation, congestive otitis.
