'''<u>IN ACUTE MEDIUM OTITIS:</u>'''

*''No indication for antibiotic treatment if congestive AOM, seromucous otitis'''

*Antibiotic treatment recommended if :
*#OMA child < 2 years ;
*#Child > 2 years old immediately if high fever, intense earache or difficulty understanding instructions.
*#Child > 2 years of age after reassessment 48-72 hours in the event of persistent symptoms.
*#OMA in adults

*First-line treatment:''''
*#Child: amoxicillin 80-90mg/kg/day 8-10 days (child <2 years); 5 days (child >2 years)
*#In adults: amoxicillin 2-3g/d
*#Total daily dose divided into 2 or 3 doses

*'''If allergic:''''
*#In children: allergy to penicillins without allergy to cephalosporins; cefpodoxime; CI betalactam antibiotics: cotrimoxazole or erythromycin-sulfafurazole.
*#In adults: allergy to penicillins without allergy to cephalosporins: cefuroxime-axetil or cefpodoxime or cefotiam; betalactam CI: pristinamycin or cotrimoxazole or levofloxacin.

*Special cases
*#Newborn: germs resistant to the usual antibiotics (''S aureus'', ''P aeruginosa'', ''Strepto pyogenes''); extensive work-up for any neonatal fever; broad-spectrum antibiotic therapy; systematic paracentesis.
*#Child with conjunctivitis: "Haemophilus" "influenzae" possibly involved; prescribe Amoxicillin + Clavulanic Acid<br />.

*If unsuccessful :

o Amoxicillin-ac clavulanic combination - to be effective against betalactamase-producing H. influenzae.

o Amoxicillin at 150 mg/kg/d to be effective against pneumococcus of reduced sensitivity to penicillin (PSDP)<br />.

*Paracentesis: risk of contamination with otorrhea sample taken from the duct

o Bacteriological study: abnormal or complicated evolution, slow-onset otitis, particular condition (infant < 3 months, immune deficiency), change in general condition

o Hyperalgesic AOM collection

o AOM collected with fever, resistant to antipyretics
