* Careful preparation of the immobilisation
* Informing and educating the patient or parents if the patient is a child
* Monitoring to ensure that instructions are followed.
* Reduce oedema: Elevate the immobilised limb, especially in the first few days.
** on the upper limb :
*** Standing: support with a sling and hand raised or on a cushion.
*** when decubitus: hand raised on a cushion
** on the lower limb: raise the end of the limb when sitting or lying down

* Do regular isometric contraction exercises under restraint, and mobilise the joints above and below.
* Ice: apply ice in a waterproof bag.
* Take care of the restraint: keep dry, inspect regularly.
* If the patient is pubescent, daily anticoagulant treatment with LMWH to prevent phlebothrombosis is imperative for any lower limb support with or without weight-bearing.
* Follow-up consultations with radiological control of the immobilised segment.
* The support must be worn until the end of the protocol initially defined.
