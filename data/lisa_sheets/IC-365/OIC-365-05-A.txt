'''Explain the instructions to the patient and ensure that they are fully understood after the immobilisation has been applied to the patient'' :

* Duration of immobilisation
* The fight against oedema
* Mobilisation of overlying and underlying joints, isometric contractions under restraint
* Prevention of phlebothrombosis when immobilising the lower limb
* The need for regular clinical and radiological checks

'''Consultation as soon as possible if'''i :

* Increasing pain
* Swelling
* Sensation of numbness, discolouration, change in local heat
* Deterioration of the support
* Stains on the support and/or foul odours
* Fever
