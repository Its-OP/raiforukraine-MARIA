*Pre-cancerous state: germ cell neoplasia "in situ" (GNIS)
*Local growth in the parenchyma (pulp); 60 to 70% at the time of diagnosis
*Lymphatic metastasis
**Retroperitoneal lymph nodes: 1<sup>er</sup> lymph node relay (except if scrotal approach: inguinal, in addition)
**Mediastinal lymph nodes
**Left supraclavicular lymph nodes
*Distant extension (haematogenous, choriocarcinoma)
**Lung
**Liver
**Rare: brain, bone
**
**
**Bilateral testicular tumours are possible but rare (<1%).
