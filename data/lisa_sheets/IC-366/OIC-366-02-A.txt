In the event of a blood exposure accident and when the source patient has an active and replicative infection:

- HIV: 0.3% infection after percutaneous exposure to blood

- HCV: 3% infection after percutaneous exposure to blood

- HBV: 40% infection after percutaneous exposure to blood (if unvaccinated)

In the event of a sexual exposure accident and when the source patient has an active and replicative infection:

- HIV: 0.1% after insertive vaginal intercourse and 1% after receptive anal intercourse (but may vary if genital lesions are present)

- HCV: low but significant risk in cases of traumatic sexual intercourse

- HBV: 30-50% infection after sexual exposure (if unvaccinated)
