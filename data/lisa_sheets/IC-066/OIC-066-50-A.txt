Exposure to trauma is required.

Repetition syndrome: involuntary, vivid, intrusive, distressing and recurrent reliving of traumatic events, day or night, with dissociative flashbacks and strong physiological and psychological reactivity.

Avoidance syndrome: avoidance of any stimulus that recalls the trauma (people, places, situations, activities, conversations).

Negative cognitive and mood changes: persistent cognitive distortions about self and others, blaming or ruminating, mood changes, detachment, difficulty experiencing positive emotions.

Neurovegetative hyperactivation: impaired sleep, irritability, hypervigilance, exaggerated startle reactions, concentration and memory problems.
