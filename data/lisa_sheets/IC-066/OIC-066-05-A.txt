The diagnosis of EDC is based on the symptoms described below, lasting more than 15 days, with impaired personal, social and occupational functioning, in the absence of the effects of a substance or other medical condition. The risk of suicide must be systematically assessed. It is a medical emergency.  

Psychoaffective disturbances :

*Disturbance of mood: [[Sad mood/moral pain SD-123|tristesse]] or feeling of emptiness persists over time, regardless of life circumstances, predominates in the morning, tends to improve during the day.
*Disruption of emotions: more or less intense anhedonia, variable from one person to another, can be observed in emotional, socio-professional and leisure life; emotional anaesthesia; anxiety; irritability.
*Alterations in thought content: guilt, self-reproach, feeling of being a burden on others, extreme self-accusation; ideas of devaluation with loss of self-esteem, self-deprecation, feeling of incapacity, uselessness or unworthiness; ideas of [[Acute delirium 2C-351-DE-A03|of incurability]].
*Thoughts of [[Suicide, suicidal, suicidal, suicidal 2C-353-DE-A01|death]]: the patient questioning the necessity of continuing to live, ideas [[Suicide and suicide attempts, suicidal ideas 2C-353-PE-A01|suicidal]] with or without a precise plan.


Psychomotor disturbances: Psychomotor slowing or agitation.

*Disturbances in thought processes: bradypsychia, negative, repeated, self-centred thoughts.
*Cognitive alterations: concentration, memory and attention problems, indecision.
*Motor and behavioural impairment: bradykinesia, hypomimia, bradyphaemia, monotone voice, clinophilia, incuria, abulia. Or agitation linked to a state of internal tension or [[Anxiety SD-116|anxiety]].

Physiological symptoms :

*Disturbances of [[Normal sleep: paediatric specificities 2C-110-DE-A02|sleep]]: insomnia, nocturnal awakenings, early awakenings, difficulty falling asleep, hypersomnia or somnolence.
*Fatigue or loss of energy.
*Anorexia, increased appetite, changes in eating habits, weight loss or weight gain.
*Decreased desire and arousal [[Normal sexuality and its disorders|sexual]], hyposexuality.
