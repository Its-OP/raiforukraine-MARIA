Early intervention after a trauma aims to reduce the emotional burden and identify the most vulnerable people. This involves identifying acute signs of distress and dissociation, psycho-educating victims about the development of their symptoms and the possibilities for help, and providing specialist psychological support. No medication is systematically recommended after a trauma. Take account of the social and legal dimension by drawing up an initial medical certificate and systematically taking samples in the event of [[Sexual violence SD-350|sexual violence]], even if there is no legal process.
