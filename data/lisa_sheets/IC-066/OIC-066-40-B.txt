A. Presence of obsessions or compulsions or both.

B. The obsessions or compulsions cause considerable loss of time or clinically significant distress, or impair social, occupational or other important functioning.

C. Obsessive-compulsive symptoms are not attributable to the physiological effects of a substance or to another medical condition.

D. The disturbance is not better explained by symptoms of another mental disorder.

Specify if good, bad, or no awareness or insight.
