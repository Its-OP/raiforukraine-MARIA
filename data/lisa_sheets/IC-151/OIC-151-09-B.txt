- Often polymicrobial in origin (contiguity)

- Oral Streptococci (contiguity)

- Anaerobes (contiguity)

- S. aureus (post-surgical or blood-borne)

- P. aeruginosa (post-surgical)

- Listeria monocytogenes (blood-borne)

- Mycobacterium tuberculosis (blood-borne)

- Toxoplasma gondii (in situ or haematogenous reactivation)

- Micromycetes ("Aspergillus fumigatus" or other rarer filamentous fungi, all in immunocompromised patients: by sinus contiguity or haematogenous route)
