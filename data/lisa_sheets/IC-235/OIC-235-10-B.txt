* Early pericarditis (D3 - D5):''''
** following transmural -> haemopericardial infarction, due to effusion.
** Most often a favourable outcome if minimal,
** Emergency surgery in case of myocardial rupture.
* Late pericarditis (2-16 weeks) = Dressler syndrome.
** Classically associated with fever, pleurisy, arthralgia, AEG, significant inflammatory syndrome, prolongation of the QT interval on ECG.
** Rare nowadays.
