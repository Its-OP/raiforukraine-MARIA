I. Diagnosing glomerular nephropathy

A. Suggest a glomerular syndrome if one or both of the following signs are present:'''

*[[Proteinuria SD-212|Proteinuria]] glomerular ''':''''
**detected by dipstick ;
**confirmed by assay (in g/g creatinine or mg/mmol creatinine or g/24 hours);
**composed of at least 60% albumin.
*Haematuria :
**microscopic (red blood cells > 10/mm3 or 104/ml) +/- deformed red blood cells or red blood cell cylinders;
**total macroscopic, painless, without clots.
**Three other signs may be associated:
*HTA ;
*Peripheral oedema;
*acute or chronic renal failure.
