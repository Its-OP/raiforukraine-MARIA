| colspan="3" |'''Sensorimotor neuropathy''''
|-
| Polyneuritis
|Hypoesthesia, distal paresthesias, abolished ROT, sensory disorders (epicritic, thermoalgesic, diaspason, proprioception), arch deformity
|Interview, DN4 questionnaire, abnormal monofilament test, EMG (if atypical).
|-
| Mononeuritis
|Description of the painful pathway according to the nerve affected (n facial, crural, L2, L3, etc.).
|Depending on the nerve: cerebral CT scan, EMG
|-
| Thoracic polyradiculopathy
|Abdominal pain aggravated at night, weight loss, wall dehiscence
|EMG (nerve root involvement T1 to T12)
|-
| colspan="3" |'''Autonomic neuropathy''''
|-
| Cardiac (NAC)
|Sinus tachycardia, permanent bradycardia (rare)
|QT prolongation, variation in heart rate (M. Vasalva, tilt test), stress test
|-
| N Vasomotor
|Orthostatic hypotension
|fall > 20 mmHg SBP, > 10 DBP on change from lying to standing
|-
| Sweating dysregulation
|Hypersudation
|

| - Gastrointestinal NA
|Rapid satiety, vomiting, diarrhoea or alternating with constipation, faecal incontinence
|FOGD (food bolus, bezoar), gastric emptying scan
|-
| Genitourinary NA
|Bladder fullness perception defect, erectile dysfunction, retrograde ejaculation, anejaculation
