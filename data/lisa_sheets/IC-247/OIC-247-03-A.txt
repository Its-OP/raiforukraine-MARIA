Diabetes may be diagnosed in the following situations:

1/ Screening


2/ Fasting venous blood glucose ≥ 1.26 g/l on two occasions.  

3/ Orally induced hyperglycaemia if blood glucose 2 hours after oral loading is greater than or equal to 2 g/l [11 mmol/l].

OGTT is indicated in the following cases:

- pregnancy

- fasting blood glucose between 1.10 and 1.26 g/l [6 to 7 mmol/l].


4/ Emergency situations in symptomatic patients with blood glucose levels of 2 g/l [11 mmol/l] or more

In children, cardinal syndrome is often present, but is difficult to recognise in infants.

Ketoacidosis is often present at diagnosis, especially in children aged < 5 years.
