-Clinic

*polyuro-polydipsic syndrome: look for hyperglycaemia
*dehydration: think of hyperglycaemia
*acute abdominal pain in children and adults|abdominal pain]], [[Nausea SD-012|nausea]]: think of ketosis
*adrenergic signs in a diabetic (sweating, pallor, tachycardia): think of hypoglycaemia
*disturbance of vigilance: think of hyper or hypoglycaemia: calm coma: hyperglycaemia, agitated coma: hypoglycaemia [[Coma and consciousness disorders SD-028]].

- Organic

*hyperglycaemia greater than 2.5 g/l: ketone levels should be measured
*hypoglycaemia <70 mg/l, life-threatening <54 mg/l, severe if re-sugaring by a third party is required or associated loss of alertness
*dehydration or renal failure, remember to check blood glucose, if high: ketonemia and osmolarity
