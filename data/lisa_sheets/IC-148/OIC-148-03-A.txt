'''Maxillary sinusitis:'''

The diagnosis is clinical. The diagnostic criteria are :

<nowiki>*</nowiki>Presence of''''at least two of the three criteria'''' '''major''':

Ø Persistence or increase in infraorbital sinus pain despite symptomatic treatment for at least 48 hours.

Ø Type of pain: unilateral and/or increased by tilting the head forward and/or pulsatile and/or worse in the late afternoon and at night

Ø Increased rhinorrhoea and purulent nature of the rhinorrhoea, especially if it becomes unilateral.

<nowiki>*</nowiki>Strengthening diagnostic suspicion if '''presence of minor criteria''' :

Ø Fever > 3 days

Ø Nasal obstruction and/or sneezing and/or pharyngeal discomfort and/or coughing > 10 days

'''No further examination unless dental origin suspected'' (unilateral maxillary sinusitis without rhinitis/rhinopharyngitis, suggestive dental examination): dental panoramic or dental apex scan (dentascan).

'''Frontal sinusitis:'''

To be suspected when :

Ø Supraorbital headaches

Ø Evolution of symptoms > 72 hours

Confirmation by sinus scan

'''Ethmoidal sinusitis:'''

To be suspected when :

Ø Filling of the internal angle of the eye

Ø Palpebral oedema

Ø Retro-orbital headaches

Ø Evolution of symptoms > 72 hours

Confirmation by sinus scan

'''Sphenoidal sinusitis:'''

To be suspected when :

Ø Permanent retro-orbital headaches

Ø Vertex irradiation

Ø Evolution of symptoms > 72 hours

Confirmation by sinus scan
