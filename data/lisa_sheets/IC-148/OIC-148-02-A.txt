Acute rhinitis and rhinopharyngitis are ''benign conditions of viral origin.''

There are many species involved, including rhinoviruses, adenoviruses, coronaviruses, RSV, i'nfluenzae'' and ''para-influenzae'', and enteroviruses.


Acute sinusitis is a viral or bacterial disease.

Bacterial sinus infections can occur :

<nowiki>*</nowiki>By nasal route following acute rhinitis/rhinopharyngitis (< 1%), or more rarely following barotrauma. '''The main germs responsible are''':

Ø Pneumococcus

Ø Streptococcus

Ø ''Haemophilus influenzae

Ø ''Moraxella catarrhalis

Ø Staphylococcus

<nowiki>*</nowiki>By dental route following the spread of a dental or periodontal infection. '''The germs involved may then be anaerobic''.
