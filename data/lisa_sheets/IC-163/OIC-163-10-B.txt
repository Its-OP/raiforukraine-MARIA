Complications" can be "multiple":

*Pulmonary: asphyxiating coughs, apnoea, atelectasis due to bronchial obstruction.
*Infections: bacterial or viral bronchopulmonary superinfections suspected in the presence of fever.
*Decompensation of an underlying comorbidity: heart failure, respiratory failure, diabetes, etc.
*Mechanics associated with coughing: prolapses, hernias, rib fractures, pneumothorax, etc.
*Denutrition and dehydration, secondary to vomiting and eating difficulties (a trigger for coughing fits), especially in elderly people living in institutions.
