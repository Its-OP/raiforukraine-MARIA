Secondary PID corresponds to non-idiopathic PID. They may be :

* Infectious: most often acute IPD, but certain pathogens can cause subacute or even chronic IPD (e.g. pneumocystis, mycobacteria);
* Cardiovascular: heart failure;
* Medications;
* Systemic diseases: connectivites (e.g. rheumatoid arthritis, scleroderma, inflammatory myositis) or vasculitides (e.g. microscopic polyangiitis);
* Tumour: carcinomatous lymphangitis, haemopathies.
* Metabolic: overload diseases (e.g. Niemann-Pick disease, Gaucher disease).
