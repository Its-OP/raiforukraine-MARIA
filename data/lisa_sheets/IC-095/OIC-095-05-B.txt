|Order=5}}Radiculalgia symptomatic on metastasis or lymphoma:

- Age > 50, history of osteophilic cancer,

- Altered general condition, progressive lumboradiculalgia, mixed or inflammatory schedule, sensory and/or motor deficit ± cauda equina syndrome

Infectious spondylodiscitis :

- Previous invasive procedure (infiltration, surgery), immunosuppression factor, active infection

- Inflammatory pattern of pain, signs of infection

- Existence of an entrance door

- Search for associated endocarditis

Intraspinal tumour :

- Inflammatory pain schedule

- Lesional or sub-lesional neurological signs

Meningoradiculitis (herpes, shingles, HIV, Lyme)

- Infectious context

- Meningeal syndrome, pluriradicular irradiation

- Skin lesions

Spondyloarthritis :

- Men under 45

- Family history of spondyloarthritis, psoriasis, uveitis, IBD

- Personal history of psoriasis, uveitis, IBD, enthesitis, dactylitis, arthritis

- HLA B27

- Biological inflammatory syndrome

- Truncated sciatica pain, inflammatory schedule, good efficacy of NSAIDs

Specific differential diagnosis of low back pain :

- Osteoarticular lesion of the pelvis or hip

- Muscular pathology (psoas haematoma, tumour invasion)

- Neurological damage (paresthetic meralgia)
