*Radiculalgia is pain whose systematisation/topography coincides with a dermatome, i.e. a specific sensory territory innervated by a single spinal root.
*Pain may be mechanical (daytime, increased by physical effort), inflammatory (nocturnal recrudescence) and/or neuropathic (DN4 score ≥ 4/10).
*Objective sensory and/or motor damage in the territory of the spinal root may accompany the pain:

*Hypo/anaesthesia
*Muscle strength deficit: score from 0 (no contraction) to 5 (movement against resistance, normal strength)
*myotrophy
*Diminution or abolition of an osteotendinous reflex (OTR)
