|Order=7}}If secondary pathology is suspected: imaging of the spine, looking for inflammatory syndrome and hypercalcaemia.

If no signs of seriousness: no imaging in the first instance

If an invasive procedure is envisaged (infiltration, surgery): imaging of the spine recommended (MRI) and biological check-up with haemostasis (Reasoned request/prescription and choice of a diagnostic test; Drafting of the request for an imaging test; Request for an imaging test; Request for an explanation from a patient about the procedure, risks and expected benefits of an imaging test).

If plexopathy suspected: EMG and discussion Lumbar puncture for CSF examination
