'''Benign tumours:''''

a) ''Functional'' cysts: disappear spontaneously on ultrasound examination

b) ''Organic'' cysts: 3 origins

- ''Epithelial'': serous cystadenomas, mucinous cystadenomas or endometriomas

- '''Germinal''': dermoid cyst or mature teratoma

- '''Stromal''': fibrothecoma


'''Malignant tumours''': same histological forms, but malignant sub-form

- Epithelial" tumours (90%): high or low grade serous cystadenocarcinomas (the most common form), mucinous cystadenocarcinomas, endometrioid cystadenocarcinomas or clear cell carcinomas.

- Germ cell or sex cord tumours (more common in younger patients): mature teratomas, dysgerminomas, granulosa tumours, choriocarcinomas.

- Ovarian metastases: 10% of malignant ovarian tumours

*Colorectal or gastric digestive origin (Krukenberg syndrome): bilateral mucinous tumours.
*Metastatic breast cancer


Borderline tumours of the ovary: serous, non-invasive micropapillary, mucinous, endometrioid
