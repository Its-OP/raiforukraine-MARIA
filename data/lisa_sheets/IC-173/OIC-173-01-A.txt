Zoonoses are infectious diseases transmitted from animals to humans either directly by the animal (bacterial: Q fever, pasteurellosis, cat scratch disease; parasitic: toxoplasmosis, echinococcosis) or indirectly via vectors such as ticks, fleas and sandflies (bacterial: Lyme borreliosis, Q fever; parasitic: leishmaniasis).
