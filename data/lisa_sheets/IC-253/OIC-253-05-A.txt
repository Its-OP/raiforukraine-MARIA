The clinical examination of the obese patient must specify the distribution of excess body fat:

Waist measurement

- in standing adults, with a tape measure in a horizontal position midway between the lower costal margin and the anterior superior iliac spines, after gentle exhalation and with the arms hanging loosely.

- correlated with the amount of intra-abdominal (visceral) fat.

- is used to define the level of cardio-metabolic risk associated with the distribution of body fat:

[Cardiovascular risk factors and prevention

''Different waist circumference thresholds associated with an increased risk of metabolic and cardiovascular pathologies:''

- ≥ 80 cm in women, ≥ 94 cm in men: IDF criterion

- ≥ 88 cm in women, ≥ 102 cm in men: NCEP-ATPIII criterion
