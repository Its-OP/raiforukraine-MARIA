'''A haemogram should be performed in the presence of:'''

- signs suggestive of a reduction in one or more blood lines:

- anaemia syndrome

- haemorrhagic syndrome

- unexplained, persistent, recurrent or severe infectious syndrome ;

- signs suggestive of an increase in one or more blood lines:

- skin erythrosis or pruritus to water ;

- arterial or venous thrombosis ;

- tumour syndrome: adenopathy, splenomegaly ;

- changes in general condition: asthenia, anorexia, weight loss, long-term fever, bone pain, etc. ;

- certain situations in which an NFS check should or can be carried out in the absence of symptoms:

- pregnancy ;

- jaundice ;

- occupational medicine ;

- screening medicine ;

- preoperatively;

- pre-therapy or follow-up.

Diagnostic haemograms must be "performed before any treatment that may alter the data and interpretation" (iron, vitamin B12, folic acid, transfusion, etc.).
