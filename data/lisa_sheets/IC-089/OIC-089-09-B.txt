'''Interpretation of the tympanogram''' in the presence of ST :

*Type A (normal): no compliance abnormality (e.g. otosclerosis).
*Type B (flat tympanogram): retrotympanic effusion (e.g. seromucous otitis).
*Type C (peak deviation towards negative pressures): tubal dysfunction (e.g. chronic otitis)
*Eiffel Tower" tympanogram: increased compliance (e.g. ossicular dislocation)

'''Interpretation of the'''' '''stapedial reflex'''' in the presence of ST: the main interest lies in the exploration of conductive hearing loss with a normal eardrum, where the abolition of the stapedial reflex is an essential element in the diagnosis of otosclerosis.
