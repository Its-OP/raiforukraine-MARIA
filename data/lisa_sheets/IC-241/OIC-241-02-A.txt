To explore a thyroid nodule and/or goitre, the following imaging examinations are recommended depending on the context. [Reasoned request/prescription and choice of diagnostic examination SD-178]]:

1. ''''' Thyroid ultrasound''''' is a first-line examination to look for nodular images, allowing :

o Assess the volume of the thyroid gland and confirm the presence of a goitre;

o Assess the thyroid parenchyma for signs of thyroiditis (hypoechoic parenchyma with varying degrees of hypervascularity),

o Confirm the presence of nodules

o Monitor the progress of one or more lesions

2'''''. A cervico-thoracic CT scan''''' is indicated in cases of plunging goitre. It is used to assess the endothoracic extension and/or tracheal impact of a goitre.

3. Thyroid scintigraphy'' is an examination indicated for the aetiology of hyperthyroidism. [Hyperthyroidism
