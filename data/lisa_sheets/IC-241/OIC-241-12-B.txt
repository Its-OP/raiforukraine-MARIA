There are five main histological types:

* Vesicular carcinomas (developed at the expense of thyroid follicular cells of epithelial origin): these account for 90-95% of thyroid cancers. This group includes :
** Differentiated papillary carcinomas: 85% of cases;
** Differentiated vesicular carcinomas: 5%;
** Poorly differentiated and oncocytic carcinomas'': 2 to 5%;
** Anaplastic carcinomas'': rare (1%), occurring mainly in the elderly;
* medullary thyroid carcinomas'', developed at the expense of C cells: these account for 5% of cases;
