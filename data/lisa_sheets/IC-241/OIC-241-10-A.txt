The recommended first-line work-up for a thyroid nodule includes:

1. ''thyroid ultrasound'' to characterise and locate one or more nodules, to estimate the risk of malignancy (Eu-TIRADS radiological classification) and to monitor the development of the nodule(s). [Request for an SD-231 imaging test

2. a ''TSH'' test to detect hyper- or hypo-thyroidism [[Hypothyroidism]]; [[Hyperthyroidism|Hyperthyroidism]].

3. measurement of ''calcitonin (CT)'' to detect medullary thyroid cancer in the following situations (family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma, suspicion of malignancy on cytopuncture and before any operation for goitre or nodule) [[Goitre, thyroid nodules and thyroid cancers]].
