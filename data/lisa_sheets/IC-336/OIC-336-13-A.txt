- Causes of injury :

o Vascular :

§ Meningeal haemorrhage

§ Intracerebral haematoma

§ Ischaemic stroke (basilar trunk thrombosis, malignant sylvian stroke)

§ Cerebral thrombophlebitis

o CNS infections

§ Meningitis

§ Meningoencephalitis

§ Encephalitis

§ Brain abscess

- Non-injury causes :

o Metabolic

§ Hypercapnic encephalopathy

§ Uremic encephalopathy

§ Hepatic encephalopathy

§ Hypo-hypernatremia

§ Hypo-hypercalcaemia

§ Constitutional metabolic disease

o Endocrine

§ Hypoglycaemia

§ Hypothyroidism

§ Adrenal insufficiency

o Residential

§ Gayet-Wernicke syndrome (vitamin B1 deficiency)

§ Pellagra (vitamin PP deficiency)

o Withdrawal

§ ''Delirium tremens'' in its severe form (alcohol withdrawal)

§ Benzodiazepine withdrawal

o Poisoning

§ Benzodiazepines

§ Opioids

§ Neuroleptics

§ CO

§ ...

o Epileptics

§ Etat de mal épileptique

§ Post-critical coma

o HTIC

§ Intracerebral expansive processes: tumours, abscesses

§ Cerebral thrombophlebitis

§ Pseudotumor cerebri (benign intracranial hypertension)


NB: sometimes a traumatic coma can take on the appearance of a non-traumatic coma (no obvious notion of trauma), so beware!

NB: low cerebral blood flow of systemic origin (shock) may result in a non-traumatic coma.
