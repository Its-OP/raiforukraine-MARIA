'''1.suspension with polyvalent Ig (IV or SC)'''

*Systematic for hereditary agammaglobulinemias (Bruton's disease, complete Ig deficiency)
*Based on clinical signs for CVID and IgG subclass deficiencies.

2. systematic antibiotic treatment of infectious episodes

early and prolonged antibiotic therapy, taking into account the patient's history of infection, active against the germs most commonly implicated (Pneumococcus, Haemophilus influenzae, etc.)

3. vaccinations

*Contraindicated: Live attenuated vaccines: MMR, BCG, oral polio, yellow fever, VZV.
*Partially effective when useful, vaccinations should be carried out at a distance from Ig infusions, in practice 10 days after an infusion in the case of substitution every 3 weeks.

'''4. Regular monitoring'': occurrence of ADRD, lymphoma, granulomatosis, etc.
