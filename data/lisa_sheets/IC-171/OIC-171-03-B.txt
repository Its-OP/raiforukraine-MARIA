Pediculosis is caused by haematophagous insects:

- <u>Scalp pediculosis</u>: ''Pediculus humanus capitis'' lives and feeds on the scalp, this head louse lays nits firmly attached to the base of the hair.

- <u>Pediculosis corporis</u>: ''Pediculus humanus corporis'' lives in the seams of clothing and bedding, this body louse comes onto the skin to feed.

- <u>Pubic pediculosis or pthirosis</u>: ''Pthirus pubis'' lives attached to pubic hair and other large-diameter hairs (thigh, torso, eyelashes), it is also called crabs and comes onto the skin to feed on blood.
