The illness begins with an acute psychotic episode in 50% of cases

Insidious onset with gradual social withdrawal, atypical signs evolving over several months or years, a drop in school results and a delay in diagnosis

There are early-onset forms (before the age of 18) and late-onset forms (after the age of 35, frequent hallucinations, few negative syndromes or disorganisation).
