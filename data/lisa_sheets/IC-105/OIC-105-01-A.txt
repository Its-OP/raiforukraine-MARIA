Distinguish between the notions of epileptic seizure and epileptic disease.

= Epileptic seizures =
Sudden, unpredictable, transient onset of a wide variety of clinical signs and/or symptoms due to excessive, abnormally synchronous neuronal activity in the brain.

= Epilepsy ( = Epileptic diseases) =
Chronic cerebral diseases characterised by the following triad:

- the occurrence of at least one epileptic seizure,

- a long-term cerebral predisposition to seizures

- their neurobiological, neuropsychological, social and psychiatric consequences.

Prevalence of 0.5 to 1% and an incidence of 0.5 per 1,000 population per year.
