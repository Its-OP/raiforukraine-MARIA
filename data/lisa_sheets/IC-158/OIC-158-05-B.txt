Sepsis or septic shock can occur in many infections, especially bacterial ones. In order to guide management, and in particular the choice of antibiotic therapy, it is important to identify the origin of the infection.

Most infections can be identified by history-taking and clinical examination (Table). Analysis of the patient's background and context is essential: a history of infection, recent surgery, immunosuppression, the presence of foreign material, particularly venous access ports, and return from travel are all factors which may point to a particular aetiology.
