|Title=Know how to initiate immediate management of purpura fulminans
|Description=Inject the first dose of antibiotic (cefotaxime or ceftriaxone, or amoxicillin) and refer the patient (resuscitation via medical transport).
|Rubric=Management
|Contributors=
|Order=13}}


*As soon as purpura fulminans is suspected, antibiotics must be administered immediately. The details of this antibiotic treatment (molecule, dosage, route of administration) must be known.
*It consists of administering a dose of 50 mg/kg of ceftriaxone or, failing that, 100 mg/kg of cefotaxime, or 100 mg/kg of amoxicillin ([[Prescribe an anti-infective SD-255|prescribe an anti-infective]])''.
*If you are in hospital, antibiotics will be administered intravenously after taking blood cultures.
*If outside the hospital, antibiotics will be administered intramuscularly or intravenously.
*The patient should be referred immediately to a "resuscitation department", with "medical transport" and additional respiratory precautions such as droplet therapy.
