Appendicular abscess :

- defence in the right iliac fossa, which may extend to the right flank and hypogastrium

- frequent subocclusive syndrome

- 38.5° fever

Appendicular plastron :

- painful swollen mass palpated in the right iliac fossa

- moderate fever

- impaction of the iliac fossa

Appendicular peritonitis :

- generalised abdominal defence

- abdominal contracture

- fever ≥ 38.5° C

- marked deterioration in general condition

- grey-earth facies.
