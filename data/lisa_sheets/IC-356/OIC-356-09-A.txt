The warning signs are :

- A pain of the IDF

- Moderate or no fever

- Nausea or vomiting

- Rare diarrhoea

The 5 signs of typical appendicular pain are :

- A brutal start

- Permanent pain

- Without irradiation

- accentuated by movements

- starting in the epigastric region then migrating to the IDF

Typical physical signs are :

- a sabral tongue

- FID defence,

- pain in the IDF when the FIG is compressed (Rovsing's sign)

- pain in the IDF on decompression of the FIG (Blumberg's sign)

- psoitis (pain on contraction of the psoas muscle)
