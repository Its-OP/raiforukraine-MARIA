The more severe the CKD, the greater the clinical impact: reduced quality of life, oedema, hypertension, sleep disorders, pruritus, cramps, anorexia, nausea, restless legs syndrome.

Since CKD is accompanied by anaregenerative normocytic normochromic anaemia in adults and children, specific clinical signs appear from stage 4 onwards: asthenia, inability to exert oneself...

A check-up for iron, vitamin or inflammation deficiency should be carried out as soon as haemoglobin levels fall below 12 g/dL in women and 13 g/dL in men, and supplementation may be indicated.

Martial stock targets are higher than for the general population.
