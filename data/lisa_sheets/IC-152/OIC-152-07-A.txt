The clinical picture of AR combines general and/or cardiac signs and/or those associated with the occurrence of complications (systemic embolisms, infectious aneurysms, immunological manifestations).

'''Clinical signs can be multiple and an IE must be suspected in the presence of the following signs:'''

1) General signs

*[[Hyperthermia/fever SD-044|Fever]] [[Acute fever in children and adults|acute]] or [[Prolonged fever|prolonged]]
*Chills
*Altered general condition ([[Asthenia SD-021|asthenia]], [[Weight loss SD-017|weight loss]], anorexia)

2) Cardiac signs

*Appearance or modification of a [[Discovery of abnormalities on cardiac auscultation SD-018|heart murmur]]
*Signs of left and/or right [[Adult Heart Failure|Heart Failure]].

'''3) Extra-cardiac signs (infectious localisations, embolic complications, immunological manifestations that may reveal the diagnosis)'''

*'''Cutaneous:'''' [[Purpuras in adults and children|vascular purpura]] (Figure 1), cutaneous embolic lesions (Figure 2), or even acute ischaemic picture (Figure 3), [http://campus.cerimes.fr/semiologie/enseignement/esemio11/site/html/8_14.html false Osler's panaris] (painful, fleeting nodosities located on the pulp of the fingers or toes), [http://campus.cerimes.fr/semiologie/enseignement/esemio11/site/html/8_14.html Janeway's palmar-plantar erythema].

*'''Respiratory:'''' [[Cough SD-167|cough]], [[Dyspnoea SD-162|dyspnoea]] ([[Deep vein thrombosis and pulmonary embolism (see item 330)|pulmonary emboli]]) in the setting of right heart IE.
*Neurological: These are sometimes the first signs of embolic or haemorrhagic complications, cerebral abscesses or meningitis:
**[[Motor and/or sensory deficit of the limbs|deficit]] [[Sensory and/or motor neurological deficit SD-121|neurological]] ([[Stroke|stroke]], [[Stroke|transient ischaemic attack]]),
**SD-120 seizures,
**Coma and disorders of consciousness SD-028|Disorders of consciousness]].
*'''Rheumatological:''' pain [[Spinal pain (cervical, dorsal or lumbar) SD-072|spinal]] ([[Osteoarticular infection: diagnostic arguments 2C-054-DP-A03|spondylodiscitis]]), [[Joint pain SD-067|articular]] ([[Osteoarticular infections (OAI) of children and adults|arthritis]]), muscular.
*Ophthalmological: conjunctival purpura, Roth spots at the back of the eye.

'''Misleading forms are frequent:'''

- [[Acute fever in children and adults|Fever]] naked, acute or [[Prolonged fever|Prolonged]].

- Altered general condition ([[Weight loss SD-017|weight loss]], [[Asthenia SD-021|asthenia]], anorexia)

- No fever

- Pain in a limb (upper or lower) SD-071|Arthralgia]], [[Spinal pain (cervical, dorsal or lumbar) SD-072|Febrile low back pain]]

'''Endocarditis must be systematically sought when:'''

- Any febrile heart murmur

- Any [[Stroke|Cerebrovascular Accident]] fever

- Any unexplained [[Prolonged fever|fever]] in a patient with [[Valvulopathy|valvulopathy]].
