The severity of a nasal fracture depends on the associated epistaxis (see corresponding item).

septal haematomas must be detected and evacuated as a matter of urgency; if this is not done, they can lead to chondritis and melting of the quadrangular cartilage of the nasal septum, resulting in significant nasal deformity and nasal obstruction
