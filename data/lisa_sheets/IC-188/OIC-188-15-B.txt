'''Factors leading to poor asthma control'''

o Exposure to an allergen

o Exposure to a bronchial irritant (tobacco)

o Medications (B blockers, aspirin, NSAIDs)

o Medication compliance problems

o Respiratory infections

o Rhinitis, chronic sinusitis...

o Psychological factors: stress, anxiety

o GERD: pyrosis, regurgitation

o Pregnancy

o Weight gain


'''Risk factor for asthma-related death'''

o History of severe exacerbation requiring a stay in intensive care or requiring intubation and mechanical ventilation

o Hospitalisation or emergency room visit for asthma in the previous year

o Current use or recent discontinuation of oral corticosteroid therapy

o Poor compliance with medication:

o discontinuation or absence of inhaled corticosteroids (ICS)

o Inadequate use of short-acting bronchodilators (SABs).

o No codified follow-up for asthma

o Psychiatric illness or psycho-social problems

o Food allergy

o Hypersensitivity to aspirin and NSAIDs (Widal syndrome combining asthma, nasosinusal polyposis and intolerance to NSAIDs and aspirin).

o Active smoking and drug addiction (Cannabis+++)
