- Natural progression towards life-threatening conditions for the mother and the unborn child

- Major cause of avoidable maternal mortality

- Significant severe maternal morbidity: in 10% of cases, progression to a severe form of EP leading to acute organ failure.

- Maternal complications: Eclampsia, haemorrhagic stroke, PRESS syndrome, HELLP syndrome, renal failure, heart failure, OAP, DIC, retro-placental haematoma, maternal death.

- Fetal and neonatal complications: intrauterine growth retardation, prematurity, asphyxia in utero, foetal death in utero, post-natal death.
