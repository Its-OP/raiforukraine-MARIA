'''<u><big>In mainland France: most frequent indigenous intestinal parasitoses:</big></u>''''

- protozooses: giardiosis (''Giardia intestinalis'') and cryptosporidiosis (''Cryptosporidium'' sp.)

- helminth diseases: pinworm (''Enterobius vermicularis'') and taeniasis caused by ''Taenia saginata''.


'''<u><big>Parasitoses, mainly imported from tropical countries by travellers or migrants, occasionally seen in mainland France (see [[item 175]]):</big></u>'''

- '''protozooses''' :

- amoebosis (''Entamoeba histolytica''),

- other intestinal coccidioses

- '''helminthoses (worms)''' :

- nematodoses (roundworms): in particular ascaridiosis (''Ascaris lumbricoides''), anguillulosis or strongyloidosis (''Strongyloides stercoralis''),

- cestodoses (flatworms): in particular Taenia solium taeniasis


In a patient who has stayed in the overseas departments of the Caribbean or Guyana, evoke <u>anguillulose</u> in particular.
