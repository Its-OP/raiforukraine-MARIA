'''Frequency of falls:''''

*1/3 of people over 65 fall at least once a year
*1/2 of people over 85 fall at least once a year

Complications from falls are common, and falls are directly or indirectly responsible for 12,000 deaths a year'' :

'''Traumatic'''

*Contusions, haematomas, wounds, dislocations,
*Bone fractures,
*Intracranial haemorrhage.

'''Linked to the extended ground station:''''

*Rhabdomyolysis, dehydration and acute renal failure, hyperkalaemia.
*Inhalation pneumonitis, hypothermia
*Scarring
*Thromboembolic venous disease,
*Psychological consequences (cf).
*Acute urine retention

'''Psychological:'''

*Post-fall syndrome or psychomotor maladjustment syndrome
*Fear of falling, anxiety, behavioural problems
*Confusion
*Refusal to stand up,
*Depression,
