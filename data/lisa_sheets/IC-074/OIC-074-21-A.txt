Thymoregulators'' are ''psychotropic'' drugs whose main clinical action is to '''reduce the frequency''', '''duration''' and '''intensity''' of '''thymic episodes''' (manic and depressive).

They represent a fairly restricted pharmacological class which includes :

* lithium salts,
* anticonvulsant thymoregulators: valpromide, sodium divalproate, carbamazepine, lamotrigine, etc.
