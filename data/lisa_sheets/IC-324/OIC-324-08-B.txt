They can be sought by

- ''Questioning the patient'': by asking the question directly without negative connotations

- '''The''' '''number of boxes''' collected from pharmacies (corresponds to dispensing but not necessarily to taking)

- The presence of ''biological markers for monitoring treatment'' (INR under anti-vitamin K, HbA1c, LDL cholesterol....

- Urine or blood tests for drugs (e.g. sodium valproate, hydroxychloroquine), taken unscheduled during the consultation.

- Treatment efficacy. When faced with a situation of therapeutic failure, it is essential to ask whether the patient is not adhering to the treatment, as this can prevent unnecessary or even dangerous escalation of treatment.

- The identification of almost constant side effects of drugs can help: eosinopenia and corticoids, hyperuricaemia and pyrazinamide, orange coloration of secretions and rifampicin; bradycardia and beta blockers, for example.
