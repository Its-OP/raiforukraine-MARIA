Vomiting is a very common and non-specific symptom in infants and children. They are most often associated with benign viral infections, but beware of certain pitfalls and do not jump to a diagnosis of viral infection before ruling out emergencies or more serious causes, particularly surgical, neurological and metabolic.
[[File:Arbre diagnostique vomissements enfants.jpg|centré|vignette|1200x1200px|ARbre diagnostique devant des vomissements de l'enfant ( du nouveau-né à l'adolescent)]]


(1) Pyloric stenosis: onset of vomiting after an interval of 3 to 5 weeks, vomiting of curdled milk in spurts after feeding.

(2) acute intestinal intussusception: 3 months to 3 years, intense abdominal pain which may lead to malaise, cessation of transit, sometimes rectal bleeding (severity).

(3): GERD = diagnosis of elimination, perfect clinical examination

(4) viral or bacterial digestive or extra-digestive infections (otitis, bronchitis)

(5) hepatitis: jaundice, hepatalgia, context (travel, diet)

(6): intracranial haemorrhage in infants: trauma inflicted, look for associated lesions, malaise, pallor, convulsion, etc.

(7): meningeal haemorrhage, arteriovenous malformation = intense headache
