The fine line between social integration and exclusion requires vigilance on the part of the doctor.

The EPICES score is not commonly used in ambulatory medicine because of the complexity of interpreting it using logarithmic calculations. Other tools have been designed to be simpler.

Certain types of population have been identified as potentially vulnerable: single women, single-parent families (particularly women), isolated elderly people, students and people on fixed-term or part-time contracts.
