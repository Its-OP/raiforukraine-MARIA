'''MAIN ETIOLOGIES''(rank A)'' of secondary spinal pain:''''

o tumour: metastases (rarely primary cancer) ''[[Discovery of a bone and joint abnormality on medical imaging examination SDD-228|(discovery of a bone and joint abnormality on medical imaging examination)]'''''

o infectious: spondylodiscitis, Pott's disease (if tuberculous spondylodiscitis) (weight loss, asthenia, ''[[Acute or chronic inflammatory syndrome SDD-186|acute or chronic inflammatory syndrome]], [[Hyperthermia/fever SDD-044|hyperthermia/fever]]''')'')

o inflammatory: ankylosing spondylitis ''[[Acute or chronic inflammatory syndrome SDD-186|(acute or chronic inflammatory syndrome)]]''''

o traumatic: vertebral fracture and traumatic dislocation (remote dynamic images, not forgetting the cervico-occipital hinge) '''[[Spinal trauma SDD-175|(spinal trauma]], [[Fall of the elderly SDD-027|fall of the elderly]])'''''

o osteoporotic vertebral fracture ''[[Spinal deformity SDD-065|(spinal deformity)]]'''''

o visceral: if back pain
