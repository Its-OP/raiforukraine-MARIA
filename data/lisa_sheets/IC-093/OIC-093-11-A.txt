==Spinal syndrome==
Refers to aggression to the spine by the causative pathology. [Spinal pain (cervical, dorsal or lumbar) SD-072|Pain]] spontaneous + provoked, inflammatory and/or mechanical (e.g. if vertebral fracture), resistant to analgesics. Stiffness +/- [[Spinal deformity SD-065|deformity of a spinal segment]].

==Lion syndrome==

*Neurological sensory and/or motor deficit SD-121|Neurogenic deficit]] peripheral, multi-radicular +++
*Often asymmetric +++
*Symptoms and signs :
**'''Motor:''''
***Flaccid, hypotonic limb loss with amyotrophy
***Motor deficit depending on the spinal root(s) compressed, e.g. extension of the thigh / pelvis (L2), extension of the knee (L3, L4), dorsal flexion (L5) or plantar flexion (S1) of the foot.
***Abolition/decrease of reflexes depending on the spinal root(s) compressed, e.g. :
****ROT patellar (L4), achilles (S1)
****Perineal reflexes: bulbo-cavernous or clitoro-anal (S3), anal (S4)
**'''Sensory:''''
***Subjective disorders: [[Pain, burning, cramps and paresthesias SD-073|pain +/- paresthesias]], sometimes impulsive
***Objective disorders:
****Hypo-/polymodal anaesthesia
****Topography of compressed spinal root(s), e.g. :
*****Lower limbs (L3-L4-L5-S1)
*****"In the saddle" (perineum + external genitalia)
**'''Genito-sphincter (S2-S5):''''
***Often early
***Urinary = peripheral "neurological bladder", hypo-contractile, with [[Urination abnormality SD-023|dysuria]] / [[Acute retention of urine SD-097|acute retention of urine]] / [[Urinary incontinence SD-103|urinary incontinence]] (overflow urination)
***Digestive = [[Constipation SD-001|constipation]] / [[Faecal incontinence SD-007|anal incontinence]]; rectal examination showing hypotonia / hypo contraction of anal sphincter
***Genital = [[Sexual disorders and erectile dysfunction SD-063|erectile dysfunction]]
