|Anamnestic
|
* Medical and psychiatric history
* Weight history'''
* '''Restriction''' and other '''associated behaviours'''.
* Comorbidities''.
* Assessment of the social and family '' ''''environment''.
|-
|Clinical
|
* Weight, height, BMI (growth curve for children and adolescents).
* Assessment of pubertal stage in adolescents
* Search for ''amenorrhoea'' (girls) or ''erectile dysfunction'' (boys) in pubescent patients.
* Heart rate, blood pressure, temperature
* Electrocardiogram
* Signs of dehydration
* Skin and skin conditions, oedema, lanugo, acrosyndrome.
* General examination
* Clinical psychiatric examination
|-
|Biological
|
* CBC, platelets, complete ionogram, urea, creatinine, creatinine clearance.
* Blood calcium, phosphorus, 25OH-D3.
* Liver function tests: ALT, ASAT, PAL and TP.
* Albumin, prealbumin
* CRP
* TSH
|-
|Laboratory tests
|
* Bone densitometry (from 6 months of amenorrhoea, to be repeated every two years)
