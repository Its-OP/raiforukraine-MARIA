'''Beginning'''

* Often following a restrictive diet and/or vomiting.
* Or secondary to trauma or loss.

'''Dysmenorrhoea'''

* Secondary dysmenorrhoea, even if the patient is normopundate

'''Associated features'''

* Anxiety-depressive symptoms, even generalised anxiety disorder or a characterised depressive episode.
* Frequent suicide attempts.
* Possible association with addictive disorders
* Impulsive behaviour (self-mutilation, kleptomania, etc.).
* Borderline personality disorder.
* Strong impulsivity
* Sexual dysfunction.

'''Retentissement'''

* Pseudo-occlusive syndrome.
* Hypertrophy of the salivary glands, dental erosions, peptic oesophagitis and Malory Weiss syndrome.
* Weight gain (exceptionally obesity).
* Poor nutritional status, even malnutrition
* Osteoporosis
* Ingestion of foreign bodies during induced vomiting.
* Hydroelectrolytic disorders (hyponatremia, hypokalemia, hypocalcemia).
* Functional renal failure and oedema.
