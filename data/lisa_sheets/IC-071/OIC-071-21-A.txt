'''Anamnestic'''

* Medical history.
* Weight history, frequency and intensity of fluctuations.
* Quantitative and qualitative assessment of attacks (food diary).
* Search for associated compensatory behaviours.
* Comorbidities to be investigated.
* Assessment of social and family environment.

'''Clinical'''

* Weight, height, BMI, growth curve (P, T, BMI) for children and adolescents.
* Assessment of Tanner's pubertal stage in adolescents.
* Heart rate, blood pressure, temperature.
* Signs of dehydration.
* Skin condition: self-mutilation scars, oedema, etc.
* Check for parotid hypertrophy, dental erosions, etc.
* Thymic state, suicidal risk, co-morbidities with addictive disorders, etc.

'''Biologique'''

* CBC, platelets, PT, APTT.
* Complete ionogram, urea, creatinine, creatinine clearance.
* Blood calcium, phosphorus, 25OH-D3.
* Liver function tests: ALT, ASAT, PAL and TP.
* Protein levels, albumin, prealbumin.
* CRP.
* TSH, to be discussed if there is any doubt about hyperthyroidism.
* Blood or urine tests if there is any doubt.

'''Paraclinical'''

* Bone densitometry.
* Cerebral imaging to be discussed (to rule out a differential diagnosis).
