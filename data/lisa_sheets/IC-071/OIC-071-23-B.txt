'''Anamnestics'''
|
* Sudden change in weight or break in the growth curve.
* Severity of attacks and high frequency (>8/week).
* Failure'' of outpatient strategy.
* '''Compensatory behaviours endangering the patient's life.'''
|-
|
Clinical
|
* Clinical signs of ''undernutrition.
* Clinical signs of malnutrition.
* '''Hypotension''' or '''heart rhythm disorder.'''
* '''Inability to control seizures''' which occur several times a day.
* Inability to control compensatory behaviour (physical activity or vomiting).
* '''ECG abnormalities.'''
|-
|
'''Paraclinical'''
|
* Severe liver damage.
* Severe hydroelectrolytic or metabolic disorders.
* Renal failure and dehydration.
|-
|'''Risk'''

'''suicidal'''
|
* Suicide attempt or explicit suicide plan.
* Repeated self-injury.
|-
|
'''Comorbidities'''
|May alone justify hospitalisation if severe:

* characterised depressive episode ;
* substance abuse and conduct disorder;
* severe anxiety
* psychotic symptoms.
