Diagnostic orientation depends on whether the adenopathy is isolated or part of a polyadenopathy, and on the overall context.

Isolated adenopathy should be investigated for tumour or infection in the drainage area.

- '''Infectious orientation''' :

o Fever

o General or local regional inflammatory signs

o Specific skin rashes (measles, rubella, chickenpox...)

- '''Tumour orientation''' :

o Cold adenopathy

o Altered general condition, sweating, pruritus, bone pain<ins>s</ins>.

o Signs of local compression or tumour syndrome,

o Signs of bone marrow failure

- '''Inflammatory orientation''': if general signs and/or affecting several organs with recurrences
