* Clinical :
** not associated with urticaria or other anaphylactic manifestations
** usually asymmetric
** not sensitive to anti-histamines or glucocorticoids
** attacks typically last 2 to 5 days
** If it occurs in the abdomen, it can cause sub-occlusion, ascites and hypovolaemic shock.   
* Causes :
** treatment with ACE inhibitors or ARB2, renin inhibitors or NSAIDs
** hereditary form
** acquired C1-inhibitor deficiency associated with lympho-proliferative or autoimmune disease and an anti-C1-inhibitor antibody.
