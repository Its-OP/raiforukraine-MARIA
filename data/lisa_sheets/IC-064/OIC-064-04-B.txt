''Bipolar disorders'' are chronic, severe psychiatric disorders characterised by recurrent episodes corresponding to abnormal elevations or reductions in mood, activity and energy.

Type I bipolar disorder is defined by the occurrence of at least one manic episode in the course of a lifetime.

Type II bipolar disorder corresponds to at least one hypomanic episode and at least one episode of major depression (BDD) in the course of a lifetime. These are the disorders associated with the highest [[Suicidal ideation or behaviour/self-harm SD-125|suicidal risk]].
