Chronic pelvic pain
|-
|
|<Sudden onset pain</nowiki>


- Date of appearance: recent


- Pain progression: generally rapid


- Type of pain: stabbing, twisting, colic, irradiation

- Date of last period


- Search for associated signs :

infectious syndrome, nausea, vomiting, functional urinary signs, sympathetic signs of pregnancy, metrorrhagia


- Search for analgesic position


- Research into gynaecological, obstetric and surgical history


- Presence of contraception

- Presence of risky behaviour (unprotected sex)
|<Cyclical or non-cyclical pain</nowiki>.


- Date of appearance: more distant


- Pain evolution: > 6 months


- Intensity, permanent or intermittent


- Time of onset in the cycle (premenstrual, dysmenorrhoea, intermenstrual)


- Type of pain: heaviness, tugging, tingling, irradiation


- Associated signs: headaches, low back pain, diarrhoea, constipation, metrorrhagia, dyspareunia, infertility


- Research into gynaecological, obstetrical, surgical and contraceptive history


- Behavioural, social or psychological repercussions
