Waldenström disease belongs to the indolent (or low-grade malignant) non-Hodgkin's B lymphomas.

- '''Definition:''' proliferation of monoclonal lymphoplasmocytes in the bone marrow

- '''Clinical presentation:''' tumour syndrome (adenopathies, hepatosplenomegaly), cytopenias (central or peripheral), hyperviscosity syndrome, dysimmune manifestations (demyelinating neuropathy via anti MAG activity, autoimmune cytopenias, cryoglobulinaemia).

- '''Biology'''

Myelogram and/or B.O.M.: presence of lymphoplasmocytes (>10%)

           Serum protein electrophoresis: IgM monoclonal gammopathy.

- '''Imaging (ultrasound, thoraco-abdominopelvic scanner):''''

search for deep lymph node syndrome, hepatosplenomegaly

- '''Progressive risks:''' hyperviscosity syndrome (linked to IgM peak), autoimmune complications, complications linked to cytopenias if medullary invasion, haemostasis abnormalities (acquired Willebrand syndrome), transformation into high-grade B lymphoma, AL amyloidosis.
