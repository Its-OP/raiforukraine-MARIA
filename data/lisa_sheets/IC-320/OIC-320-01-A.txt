-Definition :

- Belongs to lymphoid haematological malignancies

- tumour proliferation of plasma cells in the bone marrow

- secretion of monoclonal immunoglobulin


The plasma cell is derived from the differentiation of the B lymphocyte, and its function is to synthesise an immunoglobulin.


-Diagnostic criteria for symptomatic multiple myeloma :

- Presence of monoclonal immunoglobulin secretion in the blood and/or urine

- Bone marrow plasmacytosis > 10

- Presence of at least one CRAB symptom (hypercalcaemia, renal failure, anaemia, bone lesions)

'''and/or''''

Presence of one of the following 3 criteria: (1) bone marrow plasmacytosis > 60%, (2) light chain ratio > 100, (3) presence of more than one focal lesion on MRI or PET.


-Impact :

1% of cancers, 10% of haematological malignancies

4000 to 5000 new cases per year in France

Affects adults, with an average age at diagnosis of close to 70.


-Encouraging environmental factors (pesticides)
