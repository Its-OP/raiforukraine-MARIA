The '''values''' relating to a practice describe what is worthy of esteem, desirable and recommended to achieve its objectives. Norms'' are general principles and more specific rules that set out duties and obligations to which we refer in order to judge and act with reference to values.

Professional standards translate ''professional values'' into concrete principles that refer to practice, in its relations with patients and colleagues.

The cardinal values of the medical profession include responsibility, dedication, compassion, respect for the autonomy of individuals, independence, probity, discretion, confraternity, solidarity, etc.
