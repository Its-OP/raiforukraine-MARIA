Medico-social and social partners are involved in supporting people towards independence (via the MDPH -Maisons Départementales des Personnes Handicapées for certain assistance and guidance measures).

''In adult psychiatry'' the primary support needs are :

- Financial resources: ''Allocation Adulte Handicapé'' -AAH-. (for PEA: allocation d'éducation de l'enfant handicapé; AEEH) ;

- accommodation: hostels, residential homes ;

- access to employment, in a sheltered environment (''ESAT'' - établissements et services d'aide par le travail-); or in an open environment ('''SIMOT'': Service d'Insertion en Milieu Ouvert de Travail); recognition as a disabled worker (mental disability);

- social support (''SAVS'' -service d'accompagnement à la vie sociale- ; ''SAMSAH'' -service d'accompagnement médico-social pour adultes handicapé-);

- care for "people in precarious situations" (health care access centres; health care drop-in beds).

In child and adolescent psychiatry, medico-social services are involved in :

- 1<sup>er</sup> recourse ambulatory care :

o ''CMPP'' (Centres Médico-Psycho-Pédagogiques) involving multidisciplinary teams, including teachers from the Education Nationale ;

o CAMSPs (Centres d'Action Médico-Sociale Précoille) for children aged 0-6, involving a multidisciplinary team including paediatricians, with a mission to prevent and coordinate care.

- specialised care :

o '''Les SESSAD''' -Services d'Education Spéciales et de Soins A Domicile - for multidisciplinary outpatient care (including in the child's own home);

o '''IME''' -Instituts médico-éducatifs/IMP -Instituts médico-professionnels/'''ITEP''' -Instituts thérapeutiques éducatifs et pédagogiques- for the care, therapeutic education and teaching of children (IME and ITEP) and adolescents (IMP) with severe neurodevelopmental disorders (IME and IMP) or severe behavioural disorders (ITEP).
