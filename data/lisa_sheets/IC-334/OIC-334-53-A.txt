'''Interrogation of the patient and/or witnesses:'''

* anamnesis: context of trauma, mechanism of trauma, use of alcohol or psychotropic drugs
* antecedents: co-morbidities and current treatment, in particular treatment modifying the blood crase

'''Clinical examination:'''

* functional signs: initial loss of consciousness, amnesia, headache, dizziness, agitation, drowsiness
* pupillary examination
* focal neurological deficit
* state of consciousness: Glasgow score, to be repeated during treatment, with details of the various components
* scalp wound with haemorrhage
* traumatic injuries to the face
* discharge of blood or cerebrospinal fluid: rhinorrhoea, epistaxis, otorrhoea, otorrhagia
