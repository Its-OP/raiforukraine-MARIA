'''Vascular:'''

* ischaemic lesions: pallor, increased skin recolouration time, measurement of the ankle-arm systolic pressure index, reduction or abolition of distal pulses, expansive and/or compressive haematoma; ischaemic lesions may be part of a compartment syndrome.
* haemorrhagic lesions: haemodynamic instability, haemorrhage whether external or not (beating haematoma).

'''Neurological:'''' epicritic or thermo-algesic tactile sensitivity deficit, functional impotence.
